## What is Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI)?  {#slide-2}

Diversity

- Presence of differences that may include race, gender, religion, sexual orientation, ethnicity, nationality, socioeconomic status, language, (dis)ability, age, religious commitment, or political perspective.

Equity

- Policies and practices that ensure fairness and remove barriers

Inclusion

- Degree to which employees feel welcomed, respected, and empowered

Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion are mutually reinforcing principles within an organization.

## Recent Legal Developments Impacting DEI Training {#slide-14}

-  Rescission of Executive Order 11246:  In January 2025, President Trump rescinded this order, which had mandated affirmative action for federal contractors, signaling a shift towards merit-based hiring practices. 

-  DOGE's Termination of DEI Roles:  The termination and removal of DEI related roles and content from federal platforms indicates that these are viewed as preferential or discriminatory.
- 
-   EEOC Guidance on DEI Compliance:  The Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) issued guidance cautioning that DEI initiatives must not result in employment decisions based on protected characteristics, such as race or sex, to avoid violating Title VII of the Civil Rights Act.

 

-   State-Level Legislation:  States like Tennessee have enacted laws banning DEI in public employment and education, reflecting a trend of state-level pushback against DEI programs.

## Corporate Responses to DEI Changes {#slide-15}

![](ppt/media/image15.png "Picture 6")

-  Scaling Back DEI Programs:  Companies like Salesforce, Meta, and Google have reduced their DEI initiatives, removing diversity hiring targets and DEI-related executive compensation metrics. ​
- 
-  Public Backlash and Boycotts:  Retailers such as Target have faced public scrutiny and boycotts after rolling back DEI commitments, leading to meetings with civil rights leaders to address concerns. ​
- 
-  Continued Commitment in New Forms:  Despite scaling back formal DEI programs, many companies continue to pursue diversity goals through broader talent acquisition strategies and inclusive workplace practices, without explicitly labeling them as DEI initiatives. ​

## Chang et al., (2019) {#slide-21}

Findings :

- Attitude change: 

Statistically significant improvements in attitudes toward women

Strongest effects were seen in  non-U.S. employees

U.S. employees, already showing high baseline support, did not significantly improve in attitudes.

- Behavioral change: 

Minimal behavioral change overall, especially for those whose attitudes were initially less supportive.

Some positive behavioral effects among women in the U.S., who increased their participation in a mentoring program and recognized more female colleagues for excellence.

Highlights a mismatch: those most in need of behavior change are least likely to show it after training.

Suggests that trainings should be supplemented with broader, sustained organizational DEI efforts to lead to behavior changes

## Legate & Weinstein (2024) {#slide-22}

Motivation Science Can Improve Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) Trainings

DEI training may fail to produce behavioral change because it neglects motivation

-  Self Determination Theory:  emphasize autonomy, relatedness, and competence
-   Motivational Interviewing:  collaborative approach to reduce defensiveness and inspire change
- 

Implement SDT and MI into DEI trainings:

- Build trust and empathy
- Ask open-ended questions
- Support psychological needs (autonomy, motivation)
