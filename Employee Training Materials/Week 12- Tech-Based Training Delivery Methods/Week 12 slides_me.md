## Choosing a Modern Training Method {#slide-19}

19

::: {.smartart .list1 layout="list1"}
**Consider:**

- Organizational factors (budget, infrastructure, culture)
- Learner characteristics (motivation, tech-savvy, autonomy)
- Knowledge type (declarative vs. procedural)

**Match delivery method to:**

- Interactivity needs (e.g., social media vs. self-paced)
- Scalability and timing (e.g., LMS, mobile)
- Task complexity (e.g., microlearning vs. simulations)
:::

## Berg &  Chyung  (2008) {#slide-20}

Factors that influences informal learning in the workplace

Study explored what drives informal learning in the workplace, focusing on IT professionals in a Fortune 100 company.

-   Employees often learn through personal reflection, face-to-face conversations, trial and error, and web-based research.
-  Interest in the profession is a stronger motivator than formal incentives like salary increases.
-   Employees are more likely to engage in informal learning when they value their professional development and have easy access to tools and peer dialogue.
- 
- Organizations should value informal learning and invest in resources to facilitate it

- Time to reflect, knowledge sharing platforms, or communities of practice

## Sitzmann et al., (2006) {#slide-22}

The comparative effectiveness of web-based and classroom instruction: A meta-analysis

Determines whether web-based instruction (WBI) is more or less effective than classroom instruction (CI) from looking at 96 empirical studies

-  WBI is slightly more effective for declarative knowledge
-  No significant difference in procedural knowledge
-   Instuctional  design matters more than delivery  mode 

-  WBI should include learner control, practice, feedback
