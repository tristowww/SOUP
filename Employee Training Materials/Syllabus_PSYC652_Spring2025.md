**[Course Description:]{.underline}**

This course will address theories, practical issues, and research in training. The purpose is to discuss factors and processes involved in training needs assessment, design, and evaluation as well as learning processes, individual differences and motivational factors impacting training, and selected special topics.

**[Overall Requirements:]{.underline}**

This class is a discussion-based course with the following requirements:

- Students come prepared to participate in discussions and activities.

- Students attend class regularly and on time.

- Assigned reading should be completed before class.

**[Course Format:]{.underline}**

Each class meeting will roughly be structured as:

- Hour 1: Short overview lecture of the relevant points from the readings (this is not a substitute for actually reading the articles before class)

- Hour 2: Student led discussion on article readings

- Hour 3: Applied demonstration, activity, or assignment work time

For weeks when there is a guest speaker, they will speak the first hour of class, then we will have the lecture, then the student lead discussion.

This structure may not be applicable for every class and is just a guideline for you to get an idea of what to expect on most days.

**[Guest Speaker Questions:]{.underline}**

This class includes 3 guest speakers scheduled throughout the semester. To prepare for engaging with each speaker, you are required to submit thoughtful, well-researched questions in advance. Each submission is worth [50 points, for a total of 150 possible points]{.underline} across all three sessions.

For each guest speaker, you must submit at least three questions. [Submissions are due by 9 AM on Wednesday before the speaker's session on D2L]{.underline}. Your questions should reflect critical thinking, align with the course material, and connect to the speaker's area of expertise. You are encouraged to tailor your questions to your interests, career goals, or specific topics you wish to explore, as long as they are well-articulated and relevant. Late submissions will not be accepted.

This assignment is designed to foster active participation and thoughtful engagement during guest speaker sessions, enhancing the overall learning experience.

**[Discussion Lead:]{.underline}**

Each student is responsible for leading one discussion covering that week's articles and relevant topic. We will sign up for these and the assigned weeks will be posted on D2L. [The discussion leading will be graded out of 100 pts.]{.underline}

For leading the discussion, you will be required to come up with 3-5 questions, prompts, short cases studies to stimulate class discussion. You are encouraged to utilize relevant experience, examples, or some other application to help facilitate the discussion. In leading the discussion, you are also responsible for responding and furthering comments made by your classmates. [You are required to submit your questions and any associated or relevant materials to D2L before class.]{.underline} You are also responsible for distributing these questions and materials to class (in the form of handouts or a slide presentation).
