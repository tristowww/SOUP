## Leadership Development Theories {#slide-4}

-   Growth Mindset (Dweck, 2006):  Leadership skills can be developed over time
- 
-   Shared Leadership Theory (Wallace et al., 2021):  Leadership is not just hierarchical but also distributed among team members​
- 
-   Leader-Culture Fit (Nieminen et al., 2013):  A leader's effectiveness depends on alignment with the organization's culture​
- 
-   Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan, 1985):  Leaders must develop autonomy, competence, and connection
- 
-   Experiential Learning Theory (Kolb, 1984):  Learning occurs through concrete experience, reflection, and experimentation

## Tafvelin  et al. (2019) {#slide-10}

Transformational Leadership Training and Employee Well-Being: The Role of Individual Perceived Effects and Colleague Perceived Effects

-  How transformational leadership training affects employee well-being at the individual and team levels
-  Longitudinal study -- participants tracked before and after transformational leadership training

<!-- -->

- Reported observed changes on: transformational leadership behaviors, employee well-being, perceived effectiveness of training
- 

Key findings:

-  Leadership training has a cascading effect on teams
-  Leaders must believe in the training for success
-  Employee's perceptions of change predict outcomes better than leaders' self-reports

## Wallace et al. (2021) {#slide-11}

Just What Do We Think We Are Doing? Learning Outcomes of Leader and Leadership Development

-  Examines common flaws in evaluating leadership training programs
-  Critique of traditional leadership training metrics -- too much focus on behavioral skill acquisition without focus on cognitive, affective, and motivational growth

Key Issues with Traditional Leadership Training Eval:

-  Overemphasis on short-term behavior change
-  Neglect of cognitive and emotional learning
-  Failure to measure leadership maturation
