## Howard &  Gutworth  (2020) {#slide-18}

A Meta-Analysis of Virtual Reality Training Programs for Social Skill Development

Meta-Analysis of 23 VR training studies to evaluate effectiveness in social skill development and moderators of success

-  VR training programs are generally more effective than control groups in improving social skills

18

## Howard &  Gutworth  (2020) {#slide-19}

Moderating Factors:

-  Gamification  -- slightly reduced effectiveness
-   Immersive hardware  -- worse performance than non-immersive setups
-  Software platform  -- customizable environments showed no clear advantage over other software

19

## Howard &  Gutworth  (2020) {#slide-20}

Implications:

-  VR's realism and interactivity can simulate real-life behaviors and responses

<!-- -->

- Anxiety for public speaking and interviewing

<!-- -->

-  VR facilitates standardization in social cues 
-  Limitations include technology fidelity and inability to fully replicate subtle nuances in social situations
-  Especially useful for populations lacking access to traditional social skill development

<!-- -->

- Individuals with autism or anxiety

## Dacher  et al., (2021) {#slide-24}

The future of employee development

Conceptual paper critiques the employer-driven model of development and proposes a partnership model emphasizing employee-driven development

Main Arguments:

-  Trends like job instability, remote work, flatter hierarchies, and digital transformation make employees more responsible for development
-  \> 1/3 of training occurs without instructors in informal settings
-  Development is more social, tech-enables, and continuous
