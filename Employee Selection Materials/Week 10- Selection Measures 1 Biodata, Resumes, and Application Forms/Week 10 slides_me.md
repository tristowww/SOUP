## Breaugh  (2009) {#slide-25}

The use of biodata for employee selection: Past research and future directions

Examines the potential of  biodata  (personal history information based on structured questionnaires on past experiences and achievements) as a tool for employee selection.

Key Points:

-  Biodata:  Structured questionnaires gather information about an individual\'s past experiences, behaviors, and achievements.
-  Research suggests:  Biodata can be a  valid predictor  of future job performance, particularly when combined with other selection methods.

25

## Breaugh  (2009) {#slide-26}

::: {.smartart .hList1 layout="hList1"}
**Limitations:**

- Privacy concerns  regarding the collection and use of personal information.
- Potential for  social desirability bias  (candidates presenting themselves favorably).

**Advantages:**

- Biodata captures  stable personality traits  and behavioral patterns that may not be easily assessed through traditional methods.
- Relatively  cost-effective  and easy to administer compared to other assessments.
:::

26

## Breaugh  (2009) {#slide-27}

-  Relevance:  Biodata can assess  stable personality traits  and behavioral patterns that might not be readily apparent in interviews or resumes. This can be particularly valuable in predicting  long-term performance  and identifying individuals who thrive in specific work environments.
-  Takeaways:

<!-- -->

- Biodata offers a  complementary method  to traditional selection tools, providing insights into an applicant\'s  work ethic, decision-making style, and problem-solving abilities .
- However,  careful design  of biodata instruments and addressing potential  social desirability bias  (faking good) are crucial for its effectiveness.

## Cole et al.,(2023) {#slide-28}

Using recruiter assessments of applicants' resume content to predict application mental ability and Big Fiver personality dimensions

Investigates the effectiveness of  recruiter judgments  based on resume information alone in predicting applicant abilities and personality.

Key Points:

-  Study:  Recruiters evaluated resumes and rated applicants on mental ability and Big Five personality dimensions (e.g., conscientiousness, agreeableness).
-  Findings:  Limited correlation between recruiter assessments and actual applicant ability/personality scores.
-  Conclusion:  Solely relying on recruiter judgments based on resume content is not an effective predictor of job performance.

28

## Cole et al.,(2023) {#slide-29}

-  Relevance:  This research highlights the limitations of relying solely on  subjective judgments  during the screening process. Resumes often present a limited picture and can be easily manipulated by applicants.

-  Takeaways:

<!-- -->

- Recruiters\' assessments based solely on resumes should not be a primary selection criterion.
- Structured interviews  and  standardized tests  can provide more objective and reliable data about an applicant\'s qualifications.

## Quinones et al.,(1995) {#slide-30}

The relationship between work experience and job performance: A conceptual and meta-analytic review

Reviews research on the  relationship between work experience and job performance .

Key Points:

-  Meta-analysis:  Confirmed a  positive correlation  between relevant work experience and job performance across various occupations.
-  Explanations:  Experience allows individuals to develop:

<!-- -->

- Job-specific knowledge and skills
- Problem-solving abilities
- Familiarity with organizational culture

<!-- -->

-  Important caveat:  The  quality and relevance  of work experience significantly impacts its predictive power.

30

## Quinones et al.,(1995) {#slide-31}

Work experience has a positive correlation with job performance, but the  quality and relevance  are crucial.

-  Relevance:  Relevant work experience allows individuals to develop  job-specific knowledge, skills, and problem-solving abilities . Familiarity with industry practices and organizational culture further enhances performance.
-  Takeaways:

<!-- -->

- Prioritizing candidates with  demonstrated experience  in areas directly related to the job requirements is essential.
- Focusing on the  depth and quality  of experience over simply the number of years can improve the effectiveness of selection based on work history.

## Van  Iddekinge  et al., (2019) {#slide-32}

A meta-analysis of the criterion-related validity of  prehire  work experience

Further investigates the  criterion-related validity  (predictive power) of pre-hire work experience.

Key Points:

-  Meta-analysis:  Confirmed a  moderate to strong positive relationship  between relevant work experience and job performance.
-  Findings support Quinones et al. (1995):  The type and quality of experience play a crucial role in its predictive value.
-  Additional factors:  Industry-specific experience and the similarity between past and present job duties further enhance the validity of work experience.

## Van  Iddekinge  et al., (2019) {#slide-33}

Further confirms the positive link between relevant work experience and job performance, with additional factors influencing its predictive power.

-   Relevance:  This research strengthens the case for considering work experience while acknowledging the importance of  specificity and alignment  with the job requirements.
-  T akeaways:

<!-- -->

- Look beyond just the years of experience and assess the  specific skills and knowledge  gained in previous roles.
- Prioritize candidates whose past experience involved  similar job duties and responsibilities  to the open position.

## Article discussion questions and Takeaways {#slide-34}

Overall:

These articles highlight the importance of considering various selection methods:

- Biodata:  Offers potential for assessing personality traits and past behaviors relevant to future performance.
- Work experience:  A strong predictor when the experience is relevant and aligns with the job requirements.
- Recruiter judgment based solely on resumes:  Limited effectiveness in predicting abilities and personality.

Additional Points:

- Combining multiple selection methods (e.g., biodata, work experience assessment, structured interviews) can provide a more comprehensive picture of candidate suitability.
- The effectiveness of any selection method relies on its  proper development, validation, and implementation .

Overall Takeaways for Employee Selection:

- Multifaceted approach:  Utilize a combination of selection methods like biodata assessments, structured interviews, and evaluation of relevant work experience.
- Data-driven decisions:  Supplement subjective judgments with  objective data  and  standardized tools  to reduce bias and improve the accuracy of selection decisions.
- Focus on job-relatedness:  Prioritize candidates whose skills, knowledge, and experience directly translate to success in the specific job role.
- Continuous validation:  Regularly evaluate the effectiveness of selection methods to ensure they accurately predict job performance and comply with legal guidelines.
