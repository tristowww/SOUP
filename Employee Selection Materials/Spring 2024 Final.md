1.  Evaluate how both mechanical (statistical) and clinical (intuitive) decision-making impact the accuracy and bias in measuring job-relevant constructs. 4.  You are an I-O psychologist tasked with advising a retail furniture company on refining its employee selection process. The CEO believes that implementing a cognitive ability test for all positions will ensure they hire \"only the best.\" Evaluate the efficacy of a universal cognitive ability test in this context, considering the consequences of using General Mental Ability (GMA) in job performance. Recommend a selection strategy for this retail furniture company that is both practical and evidence-based.

5.  You are tasked with advising an organization on legal and ethical strategies to minimize adverse impact in their selection process while preserving the accuracy of their assessments. Evaluate the use of targeted recruiting, differential weighting, and score banding as potential methods. Assess the practical implications of each strategy, discuss how they can be effectively implemented, and recommend an effective approach for the organization to maintain both fairness and validity in its hiring practices.
