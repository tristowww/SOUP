## Scale of Measurement {#slide-3}

How a variable is measured  

Nominal  -- categorize candidates by their eligibility status (eligible or not)

Ordinal  -- rank candidates in order of preference of suitability for the role

Interval  -- items where differences between scores are consistent and meaningful (cognitive ability, personality)

Ratio  -- used to measure quantities like number of years of relevant experience, where zero years means a lack of experience

## Multiple Predictor Methods:  Combining Predictor Scores {#slide-11}

::: {.smartart .list1 layout="list1"}
**Multiple Regression**

- Assumption of linear relationship, larger enough sample  -- relationship btw predictors and job performance is linear
- Compensatory model  -- high scores on one predictor can compensate for low scores on another

**Unit Weighting**

- Unstandardized  -- assigning weights based on importance or job relevance 
- Standardized  -- predictors scores are standardized to account for scale differences, then given an equal weight
:::

## Interpreting Scores on Selection Measures {#slide-12}

::: {.smartart .HorizontalActionList layout="HorizontalActionList"}
**Using norms**

- - Relevant, local, and transitory
- - Race norming     Bad
- - Norms: scores of relevant others in group as "benchmark"

**Using standard scores**

- - Common measurement unit: Z and T scores

**Using percentiles**

- - The percentage of persons in a norm group who fall below a given score on a measure
- - Ordinal scale
:::

## Decision-Making: Minimize false positive & false negative errors {#slide-14}

![A green circle with white text

Description automatically generated](ppt/media/image5.png "Picture 2")

- False positive:  hiring a candidate (positive decision) who ends up underperforming or with low job performance (negative outcomes)
- False negative:  rejecting a candidate (negative decision) who would have been successful if hired (positive outcome)
- Goal:  correctly identify candidates who will be successful in the role (top-right quadrant) and correctly reject those who will not (bottom-left quadrant)

## Banding {#slide-24}

- Alternative to race norming

- Applicants' scores within a range or band are treated the same

<!-- -->

- Assumption that any difference in scores within a band is due to measurement error 

- Fixed & sliding bands
