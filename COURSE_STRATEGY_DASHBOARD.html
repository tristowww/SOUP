1. Selection & Hiring

  • Person-job/org fit, KSAOs, job analysis

  • Biodata, cognitive tests, work simulations

  • PM vs. appraisal, PM cycle
  • BARS, GRS, RPM scale construction
  • 360-degree feedback design
  • Expectancy theory, goal-setting
  • 17+ bias types, justice framework
  • NLP feedback analysis, AI analytics

3. Training & Development

  • 70-20-10, training effectiveness
  • 3-component needs assessment
  • Coaching vs. mentoring
  • Transfer theory, adult learning
  • Kirkpatrick 4 levels + ROI
  • AI/automation, VR, gamification

  • Job satisfaction, org justice, OCB/CWB

5. DEI & Inclusion

  • Unconscious bias training
  • DEI backfire prevention
  • Adverse impact analytics
  • Inclusion climate measurement

  • Python: pandas, numpy, matplotlib

  • Predictive analytics, ML
  • Survey fabrication detection

  • Culture assessment from the outside
  • Job satisfaction predictor evaluation

Decision Dashboard
Top Strategic Recommendations
1. Launch Courses 1 (Hiring) and 2 (PM) First 90% confidence

Highest content readiness (65–70%), broadest audience appeal (6+ segments each), strongest search demand. Evidence-based framing differentiates from Udemy; practitioner focus differentiates from Coursera. Price: $197–297 each.

2. Position AI/NLP Expertise as Premium Moat 85% confidence

Course 5 (AI-Powered HR) and Course 6 (Data-Driven DEI) are Teresa's genuine first-mover advantages. Dissertation + AIRA framework + Python curriculum = no competitor can replicate. Premium priced at $497–997, launching 3–6 months after initial courses.

3. Invest in Template/Tool Creation as Core Value Prop 85% confidence

Interview scorecards, BARS worksheets, adverse impact calculators, 360 checklists — practitioners pay for and share these. Every module should end with a downloadable tool. This is table-stakes for competing above Udemy tier.

4. Create Gateway Micro-Course as Lead Magnet 75% confidence

Course 7 at $49–97 (or free for email signup) is the funnel top, attracting managers and business owners who wouldn't initially pay $297. Org Psych content on attitudes, motivation, and culture is the most broadly accessible.

5. Expand into Job-Seeker Vertical (Courses 8–10) 75% confidence

"Taught by the person who designs the systems you're trying to navigate" is powerful positioning no competitor can credibly claim. Vastly larger addressable market (millions of seekers vs. hundreds of thousands of HR pros). Course 10 opens B2B revenue via university career centers and outplacement firms.

6. Pursue SHRM/HRCI CE Credits for Courses 1–3 65% confidence

Continuing education credits validate courses for HR Professionals (Segment 1) and unlock employer reimbursement budgets. Achievable for evidence-based I/O content through SHRM Activity Recertification Provider program.
