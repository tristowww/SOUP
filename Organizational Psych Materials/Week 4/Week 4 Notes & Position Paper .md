The relationship between job satisfaction and performance has been extensively researched, with studies such as those by Judge et al. (2001) and Fisher (2003) offering varying perspectives on the strength of this correlation. Despite a widespread belief that happier employees are more productive, academic research sometimes presents a more nuanced view.

Given this background, do you believe that a specific aspect of organizational justice -- be **it distributive, procedural, or interactional**, as outlined by Cropanzano et al. (2007) -- plays a more pivotal role in enhancing the relationship between job satisfaction and performance? Moreover, how might organizations leverage your chosen facet of organizational justice to cultivate work environments that are both fair and satisfying?
