## Research at the Group-level {#slide-5}

- Individual predictors do not always hold at the group-level

<!-- -->

- A factor that predicts an outcome in an individual may end up working the opposite way at the group-level (non-isomorphic)

- Example: Immigration and Literacy (Robinson, 1950)

- The longer people have been in the country, the more literate they tend to be in that country's language

- Cities with a high proportion of native-born people tend to have lower literacy rates ( anthropomorphism)

5

![](ppt/media/image2.png "Google Shape;354;p47")

Relationships between variables at the group level can be different than relationships at the individual level.

If we study two variables at the individual-level, the correlation looks positive, but at the group-level, the relationship is negative

## Research at the Group-level {#slide-6}

- If people are interested in predicting group-level outcomes, they can't simply rely on the previously studied predictors of individual behavior (Ecological Fallacy)

- Examples of Group-level outcomes in real-life

<!-- -->

- Satisfaction within an office
- A research lab's performance
- Overall criminal behavior in a city
- Team performance

- These group-level variables (each group has its own distinct value for those variables) have different predictors than the variable when measured at the individual-level

- Being in a group can change people.

## Team Formation: Why are teams formed {#slide-7}

- Interaction:

<!-- -->

- During the course of their work, they may have to interact with one another.

<!-- -->

- Capacity to influence:

<!-- -->

- The capacity of each individual in the organization to influence another by reason of work or work-related issues.

<!-- -->

- Need for security:

<!-- -->

- If the employer creates an environment in which employees feel insecure, they have to come together as a group, namely the union, to safeguard their interests.

<!-- -->

- Common interest:

<!-- -->

- People with common interests (likes and dislikes often come together).

<!-- -->

- Formalized Structure:

<!-- -->

- The team-based structure may be built into the organization
- Teams may be formally assigned by supervisors or mangers

## Team States {#slide-21}

21

::: {.smartart .hList1 layout="hList1"}
**Mental models**

- The level of common understanding among team members with regard to the important knowledge of the team, members, and tasks

**Cohesion**

- Exists when members of teams develop strong emotional bonds to other members of their team and to the team itself

**Transactive memory**

- How specialized knowledge is distributed among team members in a way that results in an effective system of memory for the team

**Potency**

- The degree to which members believe that the team can be effective across different situations and tasks
:::
