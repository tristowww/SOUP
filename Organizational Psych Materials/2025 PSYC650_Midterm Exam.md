**Question 1: Organizational Culture, Climate, and Voluntary Work Behaviors**

**Question 2: Goal-Setting and Self-Regulation**

HealthFirst Medical Group (800 employees across primary care, specialty care, and administration) is implementing organization-wide goal-setting. Leadership plans to have supervisors set specific, difficult annual goals tied to quantifiable metrics, display progress on public dashboards (green/yellow/red), and link merit pay directly to goal achievement. Sample goals include: Physicians must see 28 patients/day with ≥4.5/5.0 satisfaction; Nurses must complete 15 procedures/shift with \<0.1% medication errors; Billing staff must process 150 claims/day with \<2% errors. A 4-week pilot was conducted before the planned organization-wide launch.

During the 4-week pilot, problems emerged: Physicians feel pressure to rush appointments despite complex patient needs. Staff stopped helping colleagues to focus on their own metrics. When physicians face multiple competing goals (visit targets, satisfaction scores, documentation deadlines), they struggle to prioritize and report feeling overwhelmed. Gaming behaviors appeared (scheduling only healthy patients, marking incomplete tasks complete). One nurse said: \"I became a nurse to care for patients, not hit numbers on a dashboard.\"

You have been asked to evaluate this implementation and propose improvements. Specifically, your analysis should include:

- **Evaluate the implementation** using Locke & Latham\'s goal-setting theory. What aspects align with research? What critical mechanisms or moderators are missing?

- **Analyze multiple-goal pursuit challenges** using Neal et al.\'s dynamic self-regulation framework. Why does the current system cause problems when employees face competing goals?

- **Propose a redesigned goal-setting approach** that better supports self-regulation. Include differentiated approaches for different roles (specific examples) and at least two tactics for managing competing goals.
