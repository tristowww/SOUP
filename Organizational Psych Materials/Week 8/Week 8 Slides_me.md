## Pay-for-Performance: High-Risk, High-Return {#slide-11}

![Digital numbers and graphs](ppt/media/image4.jpeg "Picture 6")

When it works:

- Aligns with clear, attainable goals
- Focuses on meaningful performance metrics
- Administered in autonomy-supportive contexts
- Reinforces competence and achievement

When it fails:

- Encourages excessive risk-taking
- Promotes internal competition and undermines teamwork
- Leads to gaming metrics instead of genuine performance
- Crowds out intrinsic interest if overly controlling

## Rewards and Intrinsic Motivation {#slide-12}

Classic findings: Rewards reduce intrinsic motivation.

Recent evidence challenges this:

Cerasoli et al. (2014):

Intrinsic motivation → performance: r = .27 (no incentives)

Intrinsic motivation → performance: r = .36 (with incentives)

Extrinsic and intrinsic motivation can work together to improve outcomes.

## Comparing Intrinsic & Extrinsic Motivation & Goal Setting {#slide-13}

13

::: {.smartart .LinedList layout="LinedList"}
**Application:**

- Extrinsic  motivation boosts performance for routine tasks.
- Goal-setting  is crucial for long-term performance improvement.
- Intrinsic  motivation fosters creativity.

**Duration:**

- Goal-Setting:  Effective when goals remain challenging and meaningful.
- Extrinsic:  Effective short-term but fades without continuous rewards.
- Intrinsic:  Sustainable over time.

**Source of Motivation:**

- Goal-Setting:  Focused on achieving specific, challenging targets.
- Extrinsic:  Driven by external rewards like bonuses or recognition.
- Intrinsic:  Comes from within, driven by enjoyment or purpose.
:::

## Case Study {#slide-22}

- Really Great Co. is a rapidly growing tech startup, has always prided itself on its innovative approach to employee motivation and engagement. The open and vibrant office space has a "Recognition Wall" where employees' achievements, innovations, and milestones are publicly praised, encouraging a culture of appreciation and acknowledgement.
- On the financial side, a comprehensive bonus system is in place, tied to individual and team performance metrics. Employees receive quarterly bonuses based on the attainment of specified targets and key performance indicators (KPIs)
- The management has also introduced "Innovation Fridays". Where every last Friday of the month employees are encouraged to spend their workday on personal projects related to the company's focus, aiming to foster creativity and personal development.
- Health and wellness are prioritized, with subsidized gym memberships and wellness programs. 
- There is also a clear pathway for career promotions, though these are heavily tied to performance reviews.
- Recently, the company has experienced an increase in employee turnover, and recent internal surveys indicate mixed reactions to several of the rewards systems in place. The CEO is concerned and contemplating reviewing the motivational strategies currently in place.

Consider the reward systems in place and what some of the potential downsides of them could be. How could they be implemented more effectively or what other systems could replace them? What are some considerations that may impede the effectiveness of these systems?

## Eisenberger &  Aselage , 2009 {#slide-26}

- Performance pressure, creativity, and intrinsic interest

<!-- -->

- Performance pressure:  mindset where employees feel negatively about not performing well enough to meet a goal and pushes them to find ways to perform to reduce the discomfort associated with this pressure

- Rewards → ↑ Performance Pressure → ↑ Intrinsic Interest → ↑ Creativity.
- Performance pressure and self-determination are distinct but complementary motivational processes.
- Moderate pressure fosters creativity; excessive pressure can be harmful.

## Humphrey et al., 2007 {#slide-29}

Meta-analysis on work design (259 studies, 219,625 participants)

- Hackman and Oldham's job characteristic model

<!-- -->

- 14 work characteristics explained \~43% of variance in attitudes and behaviors.
- Motivational characteristics (autonomy, skill variety, etc.) explained:

<!-- -->

- 34% of job satisfaction
- 25% of subjective performance

<!-- -->

- Social characteristics (e.g., support, interdependence) explained:

<!-- -->

- +24% turnover intentions
- +40% organizational commitment

<!-- -->

- Contextual factors (e.g., ergonomics, physical demands) explained stress and well-being outcomes.
