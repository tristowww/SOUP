In class activity **Expanded Scenario for Analysis:**

Company X

Company X, a rapidly growing tech startup, has always prided itself on its innovative approach to employee motivation and engagement. The open and vibrant office space is adorned with a 'Recognition Wall' where employees' achievements, innovations, and milestones are publicly praised, encouraging a culture of appreciation and acknowledgment (intrinsic motivation).

On the financial front, a comprehensive bonus system is in place, tied to individual and team performance metrics. Employees receive quarterly bonuses based on the attainment of specified targets and KPIs (extrinsic motivation).

The management has also introduced \"Innovation Fridays.\" Every last Friday of the month, employees are encouraged to spend their workday on personal projects related to the company's field, aiming to foster creativity and personal development (intrinsic motivation).

Health and wellness are prioritized, with subsidized gym memberships and wellness programs. There is also a clear pathway for career progression, with regular training and development opportunities that often lead to promotions, though these are heavily tied to performance reviews (extrinsic motivation).

Despite these, there has been an increase in employee turnover, and recent internal surveys indicate a decline in employee satisfaction. The CEO is concerned and is contemplating reviewing the motivational strategies currently in place.

**Guiding Questions for Group Analysis:**

1.  **Recognition Strategies:**

    - Evaluate the effectiveness of the 'Recognition Wall'. Could public recognition also have unintended consequences?

    - How might 'Innovation Fridays' contribute to employee motivation and satisfaction? Are there potential drawbacks or improvements needed?

2.  **Financial Incentives:**

    - Analyze the bonus system. How well is it likely to be driving performance? Consider the balance between team and individual rewards.

    - How might the connection between training opportunities, promotions, and performance reviews be influencing employee behaviors and attitudes?

3.  **Health and Wellness:**

    - Assess the impact of subsidized gym memberships and wellness programs on employee motivation. Could this be improved or modified for greater impact?

4.  **Employee Turnover and Satisfaction:**

    - Considering the motivational strategies in place, hypothesize potential reasons for the increase in employee turnover and decline in satisfaction. What modifications to existing strategies or new approaches could address these issues?

**Task for the Students:**

Based on the provided case and the guiding questions:

- Identify and discuss both intrinsic and extrinsic motivational strategies illustrated in the scenario.

- Evaluate their effectiveness and propose modifications or additional strategies to enhance employee motivation and satisfaction, considering the recent issues of increased turnover and declining satisfaction.

This approach provides a richer case scenario for students to analyze and offers targeted questions to guide their discussions, prompting deeper analysis and critical thinking.
