## Judge et al., 2017 {#slide-15}

Review of job attitudes (job satisfaction, commitment, affect)

Historical Trends:

-  Early era:  diverse, exploratory (e.g., Thorndike, 1917; Hoppock, 1935).
-  Mid-20th century:  cognitive and behavioral focus (Locke, 1976; Mobley, 1977).
-  Late 20th century:  dispositional ( Staw  & Ross, 1985) → role of personality/affectivity.
-  Recent:  affective era -- emphasis on mood, emotions, daily fluctuations (Weiss &  Cropanzano , 1996).

15

## Judge et al., 2017 {#slide-16}

Definitions:

Job satisfaction:  overall evaluative judgment of one's job.

Commitment:  values-based appraisal, develops over time.

Affect vs. Attitudes:  satisfaction as evaluative, affect as experiential.

Key Contributions:

Attitudes remain central but evolve with methodology (e.g., experience sampling).

Job attitudes are linked to performance, turnover, well-being, and motivation.

## Bono et al., 2013 {#slide-19}

Method:  3-week longitudinal field study with healthcare workers; used blood pressure monitors + daily event reports.

Findings:

-  Positive work events → lower stress, fewer health complaints, better psychological detachment.
-  "Three-good-things" reflection exercise improved evening well-being and reduced stress.

## Spector & Fox (2002) {#slide-20}

Integrates OCB(helping) and CWB(harmful acts) under a single model

-  Job conditions (stressors, constraints, injustice, psychological contract violation) → emotions → behaviors.
-  Negative emotions → ↑ CWB.
-  Positive emotions → ↑ OCB.

20

## Spector & Fox (2002) {#slide-21}

Personality Factors

-  CWBs: linked to trait anger, anxiety, external locus of control.
-  OCBs: linked to empathy, perceived ability  to help.

Both OCB and CWB are voluntary, emotion-driven responses to workplace conditions.
