## Work/Life Balance Concepts {#slide-9}

The equilibrium an individual seeks between work commitments and personal life (Allen et al., 2013)

-   Work-family conflict-  the demands of work and family roles are incompatible

-  Role strain-  the role in one domain causes stress in another

<!-- -->

- EX: work to family, family to work, student to work, work to student, caregiver to work

<!-- -->

-  Role enhancement-  the role in one domain causes enhancement in performance or satisfaction in another

<!-- -->

- Ex: skill transfer, resource expansion, psychological enrichment

## Work/Non-Work Interface Theories {#slide-13}

- How does this integration impact employees?

Spillover theory  ( Piotrkowski , 1979): Experiences in work domain "spillover" can affect the non-work domains, and vice versa, either positively and negatively.

Compensation theory  (Lambert, 1990): Individuals seek to compensate for dissatisfaction or lack of fulfillment in one domain by seeking it in another.

Congruence theory  (Edwards & Rothbard, 2000): Individual characteristics not directly linked to family or work can have an impact on the preference of integration.

## Bolger et al., 1989 {#slide-19}

How stress in work and home domains can transfer within and between partners (contagion across multiple roles) \-- 

- Spillover  -- stress moves btw work and home domains within the same individual
- Crossover  -- stress moves from one partner to another, impacting the other's well-being

## Bolger et al., 1989 {#slide-20}

Key Findings:

-  Gender differences:

- Husbands more likely to bring home stress
