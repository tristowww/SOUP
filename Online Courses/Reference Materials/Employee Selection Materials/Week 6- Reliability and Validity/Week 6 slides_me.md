## Criterion Related Validity {#slide-12}

![Two organizational charts for concurrent validation design and predictive validation design. ](ppt/media/image9.jpeg "Picture 2")

Criterion Related Validity has 2 types:

- Concurrent:  assess the extent to which a text correlates with a currently observed behavior or outcome

<!-- -->

- Measures a sample of current employees and their performance on an assessment

- Predictive:  measures how well a test predicts future behavior or outcomes

<!-- -->

- Scores of job applicants on an assessment are compared to those same employees' later job performance 

12

## Criterion Related Validity {#slide-13}

Ideally, predictive is the best option because:

-  Even though predictive takes time, the temporal lag means you're measuring the predictive ability of the test
- Concurrent may have issues related to bias from tenure differences and representativeness
- Concurrent may have issues with range restriction

<!-- -->

- Predictive use a broader range of applicants, including those who may not be selected (whole applicant pool)
- Underestimate the validity

<!-- -->

- Legally defensible

## Kravitz (2008) {#slide-22}

The diversity-validity dilemma: Beyond selection---The role of affirmative action

- The diversity-validity dilemma arises when selection procedures with high validity (predicting job performance) lead to unfavorable representation of diverse groups.
- Revising selection procedures may not be the most effective
- Focus instead on affirmative action as a complementary strategy to increase workforce diversity
- Benefits of affirmative action:

<!-- -->

- Positive role models and reduce stereotype threat for diverse employees
- Enhanced creativity, innovation, and problem-solving from diverse perspectives
- Improved organizational decision-making and responsiveness to diversity needs

## Schmidt & Hunter (1998) {#slide-23}

The validity and utility of selection methods in personnel psychology

- Examined the validity (predictive power) of various selection methods across 85 years of research 
- Cognitive ability tests showed the highest avg. validity for various jobs, followed by work experience and job knowledge tests
- Personality tests had moderate validity, while biodata and integrity tests showed lower avg. validity
- Important to do a  utility analysis

<!-- -->

- Combined validity with cost-benefit considerations to evaluate the overall effectiveness of a selection method

## Van  Iddekinge  &  Ployhart  (2008) {#slide-24}

Developments in the criterion-related validity of selection procedures

- Examines recent advancements in the criterion-related validation of selection procedures, focusing on methods and practices to improve validity and meet legal requirements
