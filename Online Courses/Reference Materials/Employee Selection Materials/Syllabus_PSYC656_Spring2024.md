**[Course Description:]{.underline}**

The purpose of this course is to develop a research-based understanding of the employee selection process and how it is connected to other organizational activities. Additionally, through discussion, lecture, group activities, and an applied project, professional skills needed to translate research-based knowledge into successful organizational practices will be developed.

**[Leading Discussion:]{.underline}**

You are responsible for leading a discussion topic for one class. This includes covering the articles and leading the discussion on them and the weekly topic. This is evaluated based on the following:

1.  Summarize the weekly articles (40 points):

    - This is a visual based presentation of the take aways from the weekly articles. It can be via word document on the computer, printed, a PowerPoint, etc.

    - The summary should include how this relates to the big picture of the weekly topic. It should not be overly detailed but still include most aspects of the article as they relate to the weekly topic.

2.  Follow up discussion questions/prompts (40 points):

    - Have several discussion questions or prompts (3-5) based on the articles prepared to ask your fellow students. These should be stimulating enough to generate discussion and not just ask questions about the articles.

3.  Interactive component based on the articles or weekly topic (20 points):

    - This can include one (if it is fairly involved) or two (if they are less involved) of the following:

      - A figure or table from the articles that you explain in detail and relate it to the weekly topic

      - A case study for students to work on together and discuss

      - A debate where sides are assigned and must be argued using the articles

      - A video

      - A tangible example or example material

      - Code that demonstrates the topic in some way

      - Personal experience

      - Other (chat with me if you have other ideas)
