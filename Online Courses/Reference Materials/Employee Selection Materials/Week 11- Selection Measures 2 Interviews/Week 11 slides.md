## Typical Unstructured Interviews ​ {#slide-6}

-  Relatively unplanned and quick
-   Questions based on interviewer "hunches" 
-   Casual, open-ended, or subjective questions ​
-  Often contains obtuse questions ​
-  Often contains highly speculative questions ​
-  Interviewer often unprepared ​
-  More potential for discrimination and bias ​
-  Validity is generally quite low

## Structured Interviews ​ {#slide-8}

-  Questions based on job analysis ​
-  Same questions asked of each candidate ​
-  Response to each question numerically evaluated ​
-  Detailed anchored rating scales used to score each response ​
-  Detailed notes taken, focusing on interviewees' behaviors ​
-  Validity is significantly better that unstructured ​
-  Training interviews improves validity

## Types of Structured Interviews ​ {#slide-15}

Situational

-  What would you do?
-   Assess applicant's ability to project applicant behaviors to future situations.   ​
-  Assumes the person's goals/intentions will predict future behavior.

15

Behavioral Experience-based

-  What did you do?
-   Assess past behaviors that are linked to prospective job.   ​
-  Assumes past performance will predict future performance.

## Examples of  Clarifiers 	 {#slide-16}

-  I noticed that you do not have an education section on your resume, could you tell me about your educational background?
-  Could you share any key activities or learnings during your career break?
-   You were a bench hand at AT&T.  What is that?
-   What motivated your transition from your last role  at McDonalds?

## Examples of  Disqualifiers 	 {#slide-17}

-  Can you work at least one weekend a month?
-  Can you work overtime without notice?
-  Do you have any felony convictions?
-  Would you be willing to treat a patient with AIDS?
-  Do you have a valid driver's license?

## Examples of  Skill Determiners {#slide-18}

-  A customer brings his check book and says that he cannot get it to balance.  What is he probably doing wrong? How would you explain the error to him?
-  A client calls and tells you that she has 10 computers in a 30' by 100' room and that she wants to network the computers.  What questions would you ask her?  What parts would you need to compile to complete the task?
-  Several months after installing the above network, the client calls and says that nothing will print on the printer.  What could be going on?

## Examples of  Past-Focused Questions	 {#slide-19}

-  When dealing with customers, it is inevitable that you are going to get angry.  Tell us about a time when a customer was angry at you.  What did you do to fix the situation?
-  The job of network engineer requires a good deal of customer service.  Tell us about your previous customer service
-  This job involves persuading employees to follow our safety rules.  Tell us about a time in the past when you had to persuade an employee to do something.

## Examples of  Future-Focused Questions {#slide-20}

-  Suppose that you were scheduled to work on Saturday.  A friend calls on Thursday and says that you get to use a condo at the beach for free -- but it has to be this weekend.  What would  you do?
-  Imagine that you told a client what you would be there at 10:00 A.M. It is now 10:30 and there is no way you will be finished with your current job until 11:30.  You are scheduled to meet another client for lunch at 12:00 and then be at another job at 1:15.  How would you handle the situation?

## Examples of  Organizational Fit Questions {#slide-21}

-  Under what type of supervisor do you work best?  Is there a type of supervisor for which you have trouble working?
-  What type of work pace is best for you?
-  Our organization values collaboration, how do you see yourself contributing to that value?
- Describe your experience working with a culturally diverse group of people

## Sample Question {#slide-22}

You are working as a teller and have a long line of waiting customers.  A customer runs to the front of the line and yells that he bounced a check and was charged \$20, which caused other checks to bounce.  He then swears at you and tells you that he will not leave until the problem is solved.  You are unable to check on his account because the computer is down.  What would you do?

## Typical Answers Scoring {#slide-23}

(5)  Because I do not have the information and the line is long, I would call my supervisor and have her talk to the customer in her office away from everyone else

(4)  While trying to calm him down, I would call my supervisor

(3)  I would try to calm him down and explain to him that the computer is down

(2)  I would explain that I cannot help him because the computer is down, and ask him to come back later

(1)  I would tell him to get to the end of the line and wait his turn.

## Key Issues Scoring {#slide-24}

- Acknowledge the long line and concern for other customers
- Recognized the need to calm customer
- Recognized the need to get the customer away from the other customers
- Recognized that help could not immediately be given because the computer was down
- Was not confrontational with the customer

## Stupid Interview Tricks that Do  NOT  Work {#slide-26}

- A manager at Wells Fargo will not hire MBAs who take more than 60 seconds to scan a lunch menu.
- A CEO would not hire applicants who salted their food before tasting it.
- Holiday Inn does not hire applicants who smile less than four times during the interview.
- An HR Professional wouldn't hire applicants who did not have the back of their shoes properly shined.
- An auto insurance executive takes off points for education because he does not want the valedictorian; he rather wants the kid who sold cigarettes in the bathroom.
- An HR recruiter asks applicants, "If you could be an animal, what animal would you be?"
