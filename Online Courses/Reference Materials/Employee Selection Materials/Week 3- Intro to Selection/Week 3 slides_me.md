## Ployhart  et al. (2017) {#slide-15}

Solving the supreme problem: 100 years of selection and recruitment at the Journal of Applied Psychology

- The challenge of finding the optimal method for hiring the "best" candidate (The Supreme Problem) has driven selection research for a century
- Selection methods have changed in response to: business shifts, legal changes, societal changes
- Key areas of research: job analysis, personality testing, interviews, other methods
- Ongoing challenges: increasing validity, reducing bias, improving candidate experience

15

## Van  Iddekinge  et al. (2023) {#slide-16}

Personnel Selection: A review of ways to maximize validity, diversity, and the applicant experience

- Balancing validity, diversity, and candidate experience in personnel selection are all crucial for effective and ethical selection practices
- Validity:  hiring candidates that are predicted to perform well on different selection tests

<!-- -->

- Structured interviews, cognitive ability tests, job simulations

<!-- -->

- Diversity:  creating a pool of candidates reflecting different backgrounds and perspectives through outreach programs, targeted advertising, inclusive assessment methods
- Candidate experience:  treating applicants fairly and respectfully (justice perceptions)

<!-- -->

- Clear communication, timely feedback, efficient process, unbiased assessments

## Ryan &  Ployhart  (2014) {#slide-17}

A century of selection

- A broad overview of advancements and challenges in selection research
- Selection methods have become more advanced and complex but some challenges remain

<!-- -->

- Adverse impact  -- selection methods may unintentionally disadvantage certain groups
- "Holy grail"  -- finding a single perfect predictor of job performance
- Needs for adaptation  -- selection practices must continuously evolve and reflect changing demographics, technologies, and job demands
