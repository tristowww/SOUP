## Slide 2

What is Selection? 

"The Supreme Problem"

- A predictive hypothesis linking knowledge, skills, abilities, and other characteristics (KSAO's) to job outcomes. 
- Basic idea is to hire candidates based on who has the highest scores on measures of KSAOs necessary for the job. 

## Slide 3

What is Recruitment? 

- Recruitment is the "attraction hypothesis" before the predictive hypothesis of selection.
- It is connecting applicants to organizations and jobs
- Recruitment can be active or passive, internal or external. 
- Involves sourcing candidates, maintaining them, and potentially influencing their job choice. 

Selection is dependent on having a pool of candidates

## Slide 6

Polyhart et al., 2017, cont.

 Early Recruitment Research

Early Selection Research

- Recruitment was not mentioned in research articles very often until around the 70\'s.

<!-- -->

- First recruitment related article was about how the German Military was recruiting for WWI.

<!-- -->

- In early 20th century, the World Wars had created an abundance of skilled workers, so finding qualified applicants was not seen as a research problem. 
- In the 1970s, business and societal changes created a need for researching how organizations recruit.

<!-- -->

- Early selection research focused on issues that stemmed from WWI

<!-- -->

- Many articles from 1917-1940 dealt with validating ability tests.

<!-- -->

- Once predictors and criterion measures were more sophisticated, research topics began to expand to personality tests, effectiveness of interviews, situational tests, and more. 
- Measures of turnover, accidents, and team performance began appearing more frequently by the 60s.
