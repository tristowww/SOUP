## Criticisms of Personality Tests {#slide-23}

::: {.smartart .hList1 layout="hList1"}
**Negative applicant reactions​**

- Applicants, in general, believe personality tests are less valid predictors of job performance.​

**Trivial validities​**

- Correlations for any individual trait with job performance are typically low (around r = .23).​
- However, when all traits are used simultaneously, correlations are higher.​

**Faking​**

- However, tests still have some validity, and it may be that being able to "act" conscientiously may be related to real job performance.​
- Individuals answer in a dishonest way.​
:::

## Types of Questions with Definitions {#slide-28}

Situational Judgement Tests

SJT questions present realistic, hypothetical scenarios and ask candidates to choose or rate the best response from a set of options. These questions evaluate a candidate\'s judgment, problem-solving, and decision-making skills.

Behavioral (STAR- Situation, Task, Action, Result)

Behavioral questions are grounded in the belief that past behavior is the best predictor of future behavior. These questions often use the STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) format to elicit comprehensive responses.

Historical

Behavioral questions are grounded in the belief that past behavior is the best predictor of future behavior. These questions often use the STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) format to elicit comprehensive responses.

Future-Oriented Scenarios

These questions are designed to assess a candidate\'s problem-solving and decision-making skills by placing them in hypothetical situations they might face on the job.

Cultural

Cultural questions assess how a candidate\'s values, behaviors, and interests align with the company\'s culture. They help determine what unique qualities a candidate might bring to the team.

## Types of Questions with Examples {#slide-29}

Situational Judgement Tests

EX:  \"You notice a discrepancy in a report that a colleague has prepared for a client meeting tomorrow. Your colleague has already left for the day. How would you handle this situation? Please rank the following options from most to least appropriate.\"

Answer:

 A) Correct the error yourself and inform your colleague the next day.

B) Wait to discuss the discrepancy with your colleague the next day, risking the client meeting.

C) Contact your colleague immediately to discuss and correct the discrepancy.

D) Report the issue to your supervisor for advice.

Behavioral (STAR- Situation, Task, Action, Result)

Ex:  \"Can you describe a situation (S) where you faced a significant challenge at work? What was the task (T) at hand, what actions (A) did you take to overcome the challenge, and what was the result (R) of those actions?"

Answer:  Based on interviews and survey

Historical

- Ex:  \"Tell me about a time when you had to adapt to a significant change in your work environment. What was the change, and how did you manage it?"
- Answer:  Did they respond with KSAOs relevant to situations from interviews and surveys

Future-Oriented Scenarios

EX:  \"Suppose you\'re leading a team that\'s developing a new product, but you receive feedback that the current design doesn\'t meet a key customer\'s needs. How would you address this situation to ensure the product\'s success?"

Answers:  Based on interviews and surveys

Cultural

EX:  We value continuous learning and growth. Can you share an example of how you feel you can contribute to that part of  our culture? 

Answer:  Based on interviews and surveys
