## Slide 4

What is a work simulation?

A pre-employment assessment that mimics the real responsibilities and duties a candidate is expected to perform in the role.

## Work Simulations {#slide-5}

Assess actual performance (e.g., fix a car, teach a class, type a document) ​

-  Types of tests (should focus on relevant KSAOs) ​
-  Performance test vs. work sample (all or some)
-  Motor vs. verbal work samples (action or thought)
-  High- vs. low-fidelity tests (level of realism).
-  All the above can have good validity and acceptance ​

## Work Simulations Pros & Cons {#slide-23}

23

::: {.smartart .hList1 layout="hList1"}
**Cons:**

- Generational differences in technology
- Time consuming 
- Expensive
- Internal development 
- Assumption candidates have the KSAOs before hire

**Pros:**

- Reduce bias
- Reduce the risk of a failed hire
- Provide a realistic job preview
- Can improve diversity and inclusion
- Automated and objective scoring
:::
