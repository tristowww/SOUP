Course Objectives:
This course is designed to examine theory, research, and applications concerning individual and
group behaviors within organizations. Readings, discussions, and activities will allow us to
analyze topics such as: work attitudes and affect, work motivation, work stress, organization
climate and culture, and others. The overall goal of this course is to familiarize you with a
range of organizational psychology topics through academic article readings, discussions, in
class activities and demonstrations, and a project.
By the end of this course, students should be able to:
• Critique the design, methodology, and results of empirical articles in organizational
psychology.
• Identify and explain organizational theories and principles related to the topics in the
course.
• Integrate theories and principles in a holistic manner.
• Integrate the topics covered in the course in a real-world setting.

Discussion Lead:
Each student is responsible for leading two discussions covering the week’s articles and relevant
topic. We will sign up for these and the assigned weeks will be posted on D2L. Each discussion
leading will be graded out of 50 pts for a total of 100 pts.
For leading the discussion, you will be required to come up with 3-5 questions, prompts, short
cases studies to stimulate class discussion. You are encouraged to utilize relevant experience,
examples, or some other application to help facilitate the discussion. In leading the discussion,
you are also responsible for responding and furthering comments made by your classmates. You
are required to submit your questions and any associated or relevant materials to D2L before
class. You are also responsible for distributing these questions and materials to class (in the form
of handouts or a slide presentation).

Organizational Research Project:
This class includes an internal project component. We will be working on collecting or sourcing
our own data to develop a project that will assess Radford University undergrad student’s worklife balance as it relates to burnout. This study is an exploratory study and allows for
opportunities to investigate other variables or topics of interest.
This project will be student driven and organized, but I will mostly help with the data analysis
component. This project will be mainly designed by the students and project details will be
discussed and planned throughout the semester. Students will work together and collaboratively
on this project both in and out of class with my overall guidance. Students will present a
cumulative result of the project findings at the end of the semester in a form that is
understandable, concise, and engaging. The project will be graded based on the semester’s effort
and final project for a total of 60 pts. The peer evaluation will factor into the remaining 40 pts for
a total possible 100 pts for the client project.
