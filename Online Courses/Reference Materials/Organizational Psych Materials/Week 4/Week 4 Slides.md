## Work Attitudes -- Job Satisfaction {#slide-3}

An employee's overall evaluation of their job as favorable or unfavorable

- Are you satisfied with your job?
- Can be general or facet

- Many valid measures exist (Job Descriptive Index -- JDI, Minnesota Satisfaction Questionnaire (MSQ))

<!-- -->

- Correlated with similar measures (convergent)
- Not correlated with different measures (divergent)
- Predict related behaviors

## Judge et al., 2001 {#slide-14}

- Connection of job satisfaction & job performance is a debated topic
- Reviews models as to the complex relationship
- Research may be inconsistent in how job satisfaction---job performance is measured and conceptualized
- Meta analysis -- job satisfaction and job performance have a modest correlation of about .3

14

## Judge et al., 2001 {#slide-15}

-  7 Different models proposed to explain the relationship:

<!-- -->

- Direct (satisfaction → performance)
- Reverse (performance → satisfaction)
- Reciprocal (both directions)
- Spurious (third variable explains both)
- Moderated by rewards, job complexity, self-esteem, etc.
- Mediated by variables like commitment

15

## Fisher, 2003 {#slide-16}

- Despite the weak evidence, why do lay people believe happier employees do better work
- Research shows the satisfaction--performance link is  weak (r ≈ .18--.25) , despite lay belief in the "happy--productive worker" hypothesis
- 
- Study 1:  Across three diverse samples, most participants strongly endorsed the idea that satisfaction and performance go hand-in-hand.
- Study 2:  Found a stronger within-person correlation (\~.57) between momentary satisfaction and perceived task performance.

16

## Fisher, 2003 {#slide-17}

- Differing levels of analysis explanation:

<!-- -->

- Strong within-person correlation leads to generalized thinking for between-person level relationships

<!-- -->

- Between-person -- individual differences in satisfaction lead to performance 
- Within-person -- momentary task satisfaction relates to task performance
