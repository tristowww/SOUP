## Specific Diversity Issues -- Hidden Identities {#slide-25}

Many people may have non-visual identities (e.g., sexual orientation, religion, learning disability)

The daily burning questions becomes 'do I disclose?', and 'how do I manage?'

For example, one third of LGBTQ workers do not disclose their sexual orientation at work

Many fear repercussion or alienation

Disclosure may not be the key, it's all about the climate and whether people are afraid to disclose

Thus, as a manager, your job is to create an environment where everyone is respected regardless of their identity

## Leveraging Diversity {#slide-32}

Diversity efforts are difficult because:

Most organizations are still run by white men, and not designed to support minority employees

Diversity programs often limit the people they are trying to help by spotlighting them

Fear about fully engaging differences keeps people from leveraging them

Diversity programs are often seen as a "quick win" and not designed for the long haul

## Leveraging Diversity {#slide-33}

Diversity efforts can be successful when:

Managers leverage rather than manage diversity

See difference -- identify differences that are strategically important to your organization and its goals

Understand difference -- gather and integrate comprehensive and accurate information regarding the difference

Engage difference -- provide a vision and lead the coordination of efforts among various different contingencies towards your vision

Leverage difference -- a stage of fully integrated culture where engaging in the previous steps is part of the culture and done automatically
