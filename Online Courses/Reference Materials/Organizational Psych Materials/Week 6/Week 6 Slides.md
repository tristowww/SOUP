## Culture vs. Climate  {#slide-7}

-  Early  culture research  → qualitative, insider (emic), historical; emphasized deep assumptions & meaning.
-  Early  climate research  → quantitative, outsider (etic), snapshot of shared perceptions.
-  Denison (1996): difference may be  perspective not phenomenon  --- both examine internal social-psychological environment.
-  Integration trend: mixed-methods & multi-level approaches blur old boundaries.

## Schneider, 1987 {#slide-23}

The people make the place

- Organizations are functions of the people in them
- Attraction-Selection-Attrition (ASA) model

<!-- -->

- People are attracted to organizations that the perceive cultural fit in
- Organizations are more likely to select individuals that fit in that culture
- Individuals who no longer fit or don't fit are likely to leave or be terminated

23

## ASA Cycle & Culture {#slide-24}

-  Attraction → Selection → Attrition filters for cultural fit.
-  Over time: homogeneity → reinforces existing climate & culture.
-  Explains why culture change is difficult without altering selection & retention practices.
-  Implications for: leadership pipelines, diversity & inclusion, and organizational change

## Denison, 1996 {#slide-26}

Climate vs. Culture

- There are differences because these are complex and try to capture many organizational phenomenon
- Culture  = deep, enduring values and assumptions that guide how members perceive & respond to their work environment.
- Climate  = shared perceptions about policies, practices, and procedures --- more surface-level and malleable.

26

## Denison, 1996 {#slide-27}

-  Culture is rooted in meaning and values; harder to change, evolves over time.
-  Climate reflects the "mood" or "snapshot" of the organization at a given time.
-  Denison argues: Culture drives behavior → climate is often a manifestation of culture but can also be influenced by immediate leadership actions.

## Hartnell et al., 2019 {#slide-29}

Key Findings:

-  Culture  strongly correlates  with other system elements (strategy, structure, leadership, HR practices).
-  Clan & Adhocracy  → support  exploration strategies  (innovation, adaptability).
-  Hierarchy  → supports  exploitation strategies  (efficiency, stability).
-  Culture predicts  unique variance in effectiveness  beyond leadership & HR systems --- but predictive strength varies by outcome.
