1.  **Definition:**

    - **Organizational Culture:** Refers to the underlying values, beliefs, and principles that serve as a foundation for an organization\'s management system as well as the set of management practices and behaviors that both exemplify and reinforce those basic principles. It\'s often described as \"the way things are done around here.\"

    - **Organizational Climate:** Describes employees\' perceptions about the extent to which both the work environment and broader organizational environment support employee health and well-being. It\'s often described as \"how it feels to work here.\"

2.  **Duration & Stability:**

    - **Culture:** More stable and deeply embedded in the organization. It develops over time from the cumulative traits of the people the organization hires and retains. Changing culture is often a long and challenging process.

    - **Climate:** More susceptible to change and can be influenced in the short term by leadership or management practices.

3.  **Level of Depth:**

    - **Culture:** Deep-level, rooted in values, beliefs, and assumptions. It\'s often unconscious and taken for granted.

    - **Climate:** Surface-level, based on perceptions and attitudes arising from the policies, practices, and procedures employees observe being rewarded, supported, and expected.

4.  **Measurement:**

    - **Culture:** Traditionally studied using qualitative methods such as ethnography, interviews, and observations.

    - **Climate:** Traditionally measured using quantitative methods, such as surveys, to gauge employee perceptions of the work environment.

5.  **Origin & Formation:**

    - **Culture:** Emerges over time and is influenced by leadership style, organizational history, policies, and practices, as well as external societal norms.

    - **Climate:** Formed by the current organizational practices, policies, and procedures and how they are perceived and interpreted by employees.

6.  **Change:**

    - **Culture:** Requires deeper, systemic interventions and longer durations to change.

    - **Climate:** Can be influenced and shifted more easily by organizational interventions or changes in management.

In essence, while culture pertains to the ingrained way of working and the shared values within an organization, climate refers to the prevailing atmosphere or \"mood\" within the organization at a given time. Both are crucial for understanding organizational behavior and dynamics, and they often interact in complex ways.

- Culture: refers to the deep structure of organizations, which is rooted in the values, beliefs, and assumptions held by organizational members. Meaning is established through socialization to a variety of identity groups that con verge in the workplace. Interaction reproduces a symbolic world that gives culture both a great stability and a certain precarious and fragile nature rooted in the dependence of the system on individual cognition and action.

- Climate: , in contrast, portrays organizational environments as being rooted in the organization\'s value system, but tends to present these social environments in relatively static terms, describing them in terms of a fixed (and broadly applicable) set of dimensions. Thus, climate is often considered as relatively temporary, subject to direct control, and largely limited to those aspects of the social environment that are consciously perceived by organizational members.

**Organizational Culture**

1.  **Deep-Rooted:** This refers to the fundamental beliefs, values, and assumptions that are shared by members of an organization. It\'s like the personality of the company, formed over time.

2.  **Created through Socialization:** People learn and absorb this culture through interactions with various groups within the organization. It\'s like learning the unwritten rules and ways of doing things in that company.

3.  **Dynamic yet Fragile:** The culture is both stable and fragile. It is continuously shaped by interactions and individuals\' actions but can be delicate and easily influenced or disrupted by changes in people\'s beliefs or actions.

**Organizational Climate**

1.  **Based on the Value System:** This term refers to the immediate work environment of an organization, which stems from the company\'s values, but is more about how these values are expressed in the daily operations and policies.

2.  **Static Description:** Climate tends to describe the current state of the organization, often using a specific set of criteria that can be applied broadly. It\'s like taking a snapshot of the work environment at a particular time.

3.  **Temporary and Controllable:** The climate can change relatively quickly, and can often be directly influenced or controlled through organizational policies and strategies. It is more about what people consciously perceive in their day-to-day working life.

So, in simpler terms, the culture is like the deep-seated personality of a company, formed over time through shared beliefs and values, while the climate is more like the current mood or atmosphere in the company, which can be more easily influenced or changed.
