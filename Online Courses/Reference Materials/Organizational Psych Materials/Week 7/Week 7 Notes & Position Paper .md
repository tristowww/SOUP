Three seminal works - by Locke & Latham (2002), Neal, Ballard, & Vancouver (2017), and Baumeister, Vohs, & Tice (2007) - offer nuanced perspectives on goal setting, dynamic self-regulation, and the strength model of self-control respectively.

**The Articles:**

1.  **Locke & Latham (2002)** propose a comprehensive theory accentuating the pivotal role of goal setting and task motivation in enhancing employee performance.

2.  **Neal, Ballard, & Vancouver (2017)** delve into dynamic self-regulation, offering insights on the complexity of balancing multiple goals and the adaptative mechanisms individuals employ.

3.  **Baumeister, Vohs, & Tice (2007)** introduce the strength model of self-control, analogizing self-control to a muscle that can be strengthened or depleted.
