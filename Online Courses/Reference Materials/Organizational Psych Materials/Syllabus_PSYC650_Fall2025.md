**[Course Objectives:]{.underline}**

This course is designed to examine theory, research, and applications concerning individual and group behaviors within organizations. Readings, discussions, and activities will allow us to analyze topics such as: **work attitudes and affect, work motivation, work stress, organization climate and culture, and others.** The overall goal of this course is to familiarize you with a range of organizational psychology topics through academic article readings, discussions, in class activities and demonstrations, and a project.

**[By the end of this course, students should be able to:]{.underline}**

- Critique the design, methodology, and results of empirical articles in organizational psychology.

- Identify and explain organizational theories and principles related to the topics in the course.

- Integrate theories and principles in a holistic manner.

- Integrate the topics covered in the course in a real-world setting.

**[Midterm and Final Exams:]{.underline}**

There will be a midterm and final exam in this course. Theses exams are intended to prepare your writing and comprehension skills for our comprehensive exam in your second year of the program. The midterm will be assigned some time in the middle of the semester, and the final will be given during finals week. [It will be available on D2L on Wednesday December, 3 @ 5 PM and will be due the following Wednesday December, 10 @ 5 PM on D2L.]{.underline}

For each exam, you will have a week to complete a take home set of 2-3 question or mini cases studies and asked to write a response or answer to each of these questions using 2-3 pages each double spaced in APA format with in-text citations and a reference page.

The midterm will be based on the material covered up to that point and the final exam will be based on the cumulation of materials covered in class. You have open access to all class materials during the exam. Working together or asking questions about the exam during the time it is passed out, until it is submitted, will not be tolerated. Collaboratively completed exams will result in a grade of zero.
