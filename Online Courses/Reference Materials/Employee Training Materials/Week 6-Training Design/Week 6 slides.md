## Donovan &  Radosevich  (1999) {#slide-19}

A meta-analytics review of the distribution of practice effect: Now you see it, now you don't

-  Examined the effect of  distributing practice (spaced learning)  compared to  massed practice (cramming)  on learning and retention
-  Results showed a significant overall advantage for distributed practice, especially for long-term retention

<!-- -->

- Effect size varied across studies, indicating moderating factors

<!-- -->

-  Moderators: Task complexity, initial learning level, and use of retrieval practice during spaced sessions influenced the strength of the distributed practice effect.
- 

19

## Donovan &  Radosevich  (1999) {#slide-20}

-  Distributed practice: offers a distinct advantage for long-term retention but this effect is impacted by moderators

<!-- -->

- Spaced out learning sessions with opportunities for retrieval practice can optimize knowledge retention

<!-- -->

-  Implications: break down complex tasks into smaller, manageable modules delivered over time. Integrate activities like quizzes, summaries, or spaced out retrieval practice exercises to reinforce learning between sessions.

## Towler &  Dipboye  (2001) {#slide-23}

Effects of trainer expressiveness, organization, and trainee goal orientation on training outcomes

-  How do trainer expressiveness, lecture organization, and trainee goal orientation affect training recall and problem-solving performance.
-  Recall performance:

<!-- -->

- Expressive & well-organized lectures → best recall results.
- Inexpressive, disorganized lectures → worst recall. 

-  Problem Solving performance

<!-- -->

- Problem-Solving Performance: Low Mastery Trainees:  Expressiveness and organization did not significantly impact performance.
- High Mastery Trainees:   Problem-solving performance was worse  when the lecture was highly organized but inexpressive.
-

## Towler &  Dipboye  (2001) {#slide-24}

Trainee Goal Orientation Effects:

-  High Mastery Trainees  (focused on learning for self-improvement):
