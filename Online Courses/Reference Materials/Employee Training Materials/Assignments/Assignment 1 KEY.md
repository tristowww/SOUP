> **[Training Roadmap: Conflict Resolution Training for Customer Service Representatives]{.mark}**
>
> **[Training Overview & Learning Objectives]{.mark}**
>
> [The **primary objective** of this training is to ensure that customer service representatives can confidently handle and de-escalate customer conflicts using structured communication techniques. By the end of the training, trainees should be able to:]{.mark}

- [Recognize early signs of customer dissatisfaction and frustration.]{.mark}

- [Apply active listening and de-escalation strategies to resolve customer concerns.]{.mark}

- [Follow company policies while maintaining a professional and empathetic approach to customer complaints.]{.mark}

> **[Intermediate Learning Goals & Training Activities]{.mark}**

1.  **[Understanding Customer Frustration & Conflict Triggers]{.mark}**

    - [**Activity:** Interactive case studies and group discussion on real-world customer complaints.]{.mark}

    - [**Outcome:** Trainees identify common triggers for customer dissatisfaction and analyze appropriate responses.]{.mark}

2.  **[Active Listening & De-escalation Strategies]{.mark}**

    - [**Activity:** Role-playing exercises where trainees practice responding to different customer complaint scenarios.]{.mark}

    - [**Outcome:** Trainees demonstrate effective listening skills and apply de-escalation techniques in simulated interactions.]{.mark}

3.  **[Policy Application & Professional Communication]{.mark}**

    - [**Activity:** Policy quiz and simulated customer service calls using company guidelines.]{.mark}

    - [**Outcome:** Trainees practice applying company policies while maintaining professionalism in challenging situations.]{.mark}

> **[Training Duration & Milestones]{.mark}**
>
> [The training will be delivered in a **blended learning format** over a **one-week period**, with a combination of classroom instruction, role-playing exercises, and hands-on customer interactions.]{.mark}

- [**Day 1-2:** Theory (Understanding customer psychology, active listening techniques).]{.mark}

- [**Day 3-4:** Practical application (Role-playing, customer complaint handling).]{.mark}

- [**Day 5:** Assessment and certification (Final evaluation through simulated scenarios).]{.mark}

3.  **[Training Design Considerations]{.mark}**

[Consider how the training can be structured to best promote learning and transfer of training outcomes.]{.mark}

- [What environment will the training take place in (on-site or off-site) and how will this format match with the type of training?]{.mark}

> [The **training environment** will be **on-site, using on-the-job training (OJT) methods** to reinforce learning in real-world conditions. This format ensures that trainees apply their skills immediately under supervision, improving knowledge retention and skill acquisition.]{.mark}

- [What are one or two specific activities planned for the training?]{.mark}

> **[On-Site Training Structure:]{.mark}**

1.  **[Shadowing Experienced Employees:]{.mark}**

    - [Trainees will spend the first two days observing senior customer service representatives handling real customer complaints.]{.mark}

    - [**Justification:** Learning by observation helps trainees understand company expectations and see conflict resolution strategies in action.]{.mark}

2.  **[Guided Practice with Real Customers:]{.mark}**

    - [Trainees will begin handling actual customer interactions under supervision.]{.mark}

    - [Supervisors will provide real-time feedback and coaching.]{.mark}

    - [**Justification:** This method bridges theory and practice, allowing trainees to refine their skills in a controlled environment.]{.mark}

3.  **[Gradual Transition to Full Independence:]{.mark}**

    - [After guided practice, trainees will handle customers independently but with post-interaction feedback from a mentor.]{.mark}

    - [**Milestone:** By the end of the week, trainees will be assessed on their ability to resolve customer issues efficiently.]{.mark}

5.  **[Training Practice and Knowledge Retention]{.mark}**

[Identify how to best keep trainees practicing and retaining the outcomes of your training.]{.mark}

[Use **Donovan & Radosevich (1999)** findings on **distributed vs. massed practice**:]{.mark}

- [Will you use spaced learning? If so, how?]{.mark}

> [Training will incorporate **distributed practice** rather than cramming all content into a single day. Key concepts will be revisited throughout the **one-week program**, with **mini-assessments and spaced retrieval activities** to improve long-term retention.]{.mark}

- [How will you help trainees retain the training content (e.g., microlearning, mnemonic devices, post-training assignments, resources)?]{.mark}

>  **Microlearning:** Short video clips demonstrating effective and ineffective customer interactions will be sent via email post-training.
>
>  **Mnemonic Devices:** Simple acronyms (e.g., "LEAD: Listen, Empathize, Apologize, Deliver a solution") will help trainees recall de-escalation techniques.
>
>  **Post-training Assignments:**

- Trainees will submit a **self-reflection journal** on how they handled real-world customer complaints.

- **Supervisors will conduct check-ins** after 30 and 60 days to assess skill retention.
