## A non-exhaustive typology of training {#slide-8}

Leadership development can take on many forms

Large scale (one size fits all)

Moderate scale (slight variations)

Small scale (mostly customized)

## Large Scale Development {#slide-9}

Lots of people, fewer trainings

Often done as a group and directed at emerging leaders

Consists of more basic 'what is leadership' along with self-assessments that are not debriefed much

## Truist  Leadership Example {#slide-10}

2 days (3 hours per day)

Take some self-assessments beforehand

Lecture-based with audience participation

No follow-up later on

Little attention paid to individual/organization-specific issues

## Moderate Scale Development {#slide-11}

Fewer people, multiple trainings

Allows for scaffolding, homework,  etc

Introduce different facets of leadership

Self-assessments with individualized feedback/discussion

Allow individuals to develop and review their own plan

## Small Scale Development {#slide-12}

Often in the realm of 'executive coaching'

One-on-one with regular meetings over time

Start with same assessments but tailor each session to individual/organization specific issues

## Denison Example {#slide-13}

Gave large scale leadership assessments to organizations

Followed up with long-term individual conversations with key leaders

Incorporated context (culture) and individual goals

## So..you  want to be a leadership coach... {#slide-14}

Hard to do early career

Steps to take

Get basic I/O skills (do hiring, assessments)

Get experience in the talent management space (performance)

Develop your own network outside of your org.
