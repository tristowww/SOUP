## Noe, 2023 - Chapter 4 {#slide-2}

Learning: 

- Learning refers to a relatively permanent change in human capabilities that can include knowledge, skills, attitudes, behaviors, and competencies that are not the result of growth processes. 
- A key part of learning is that trainees commit to memory (i.e., remember) what they have learned and recall it

Transfer of Training:

- Transfer of training refers to trainees effectively and continually applying what they have learned in training to their jobs.

## Casico and Aguinis, 2018 - Chapter 15 Implications for motivating trainers {#slide-6}

Make the objectives of the training program clear at the outset.

Set goals that are challenging and difficult enough that the trainees can derive personal satisfaction from achieving them, but not so difficult that they are perceived as impossible to reach.

Supplement the ultimate goal of finishing the program with subgoals during training, such as trainer evaluations, work-sample tests, and periodic quizzes. As trainees clear each hurdle, their confidence about attaining the ultimate goal increases.

## Huang et al. (2017) {#slide-9}

- Voluntary training for open skills:

<!-- -->

- Trainees differed in how they initially tried to use what they learned and how much they improved over time. 
- Difference in initial attempts were linked to how confident they felt after training. 
- Changes in their application of what they learned was connected to their motivation to apply it. 

<!-- -->

- Distribution of trainees' attitudes towards mastery:

<!-- -->

- Level of mastery and consistency predicted their confidence after training and their motivation to apply what was learned. 

<!-- -->

- The distribution indirectly affected both initial attempts and changes in application of training material. 

## Smith-Jentsch et al. (2001) {#slide-10}

- Findings on training in teams:

<!-- -->

- If the team leader gives informal support, it helps employees perform better after trainings.
- If the leader isn't supportive, employees don't do as well. 
- Employees feelings about the support of the team and the leaders behavior affects how they use what they learn. 
- People who are naturally assertive think their team supports assertiveness more, especially if the leader is supportive. 
- People who have less control over their lives care more about supportive teams.
