## Huang & Jo (2016) {#slide-27}

Comparison of the Influences of Structured On-the-Job Training and Classroom Training Approaches on Trainees' Motivation to Learn

impact of  structured on-the-job training (S-OJT)  and  classroom training  on trainees'  motivation to learn  and  learning performance  using hair-coloring vocational students in Taiwan.

Findings:

-  S-OJT significantly outperformed classroom training  in boosting both learning motivation and performance.
-  Trainees with  lower initial motivation  especially benefited from S-OJT.
-  S-OJT aligns more closely with  social learning theory  and actively engages trainees through  modeling, practice, feedback, and performance evaluation.
-  Classroom training, being presentation-based, tends to result in  passive learning  and limits trainees' ability to retain and reproduce learned behaviors.

## Klein, Noe, & Wang (2006) {#slide-28}

Motivation to Learn and Course Outcomes: The Impact of Delivery Mode, Learning Goal Orientation, and Perceived Barriers and Enablers

Explores how  delivery mode  (classroom vs. blended),  learning goal orientation (LGO) , and  perceived barriers/enablers  affect  motivation to learn  and course outcomes (satisfaction, metacognition, grades) in a quasi-experiment with 600 university students.

Findings:

-  Blended learning  results in  higher motivation to learn  than traditional classroom training.
-  Learners with high LGO  show significantly greater motivation to learn.
-  Perceived enablers  (supportive environments, tech ease of use) correlate positively with motivation, whereas perceived barriers (lack of support, confusing interfaces) suppress motivation.

## Salas, Nichols, & Driskell (2007) {#slide-29}

Testing Three Team Training Strategies in Intact Teams: A Meta-Analysis

E valuates the  effectiveness of team training strategies  and identifies which components contribute most to team performance.

Findings: 

-  Overall, team training improves performance  with a small-to-moderate effect size.
- Coordination and adaptation training  showed the strongest and most consistent effects on  performance.
-  Interventions that  reduce communication needs  and improve  anticipatory coordination  are especially effective.
-  Cross-training and self-correction have value, but are most effective when combined with coordination training.
