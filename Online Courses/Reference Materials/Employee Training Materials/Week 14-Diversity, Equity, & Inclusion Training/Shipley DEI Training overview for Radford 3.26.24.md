## Common Types of Bias {#slide-29}

Similarity-attraction bias:  The tendency to more easily and deeply connect with people who "look and feel" like ourselves

Familiarity bias:  A preference for things that we are familiar with

Confirmation bias:  Seeking or interpreting information that is partial to existing beliefs

Halo/Horns effect : The tendency to allow one specific trait of a person to positively influence our judgment of their other traits

Groupthink : When the desire for group harmony overrides rational decision making

## The Power of V E N {#slide-31}

V -

VISUALIZE situations  before they happen; change your mental picture and open yourself up to possibilities

E -

EXAMINE your own behavior --  ask yourself if this person looked like me (or didn't look like me), is this how I would handle this interaction?

N -

NORMALIZE --  make a conscious effort to expose yourself and those around you to people and situations that are not in your norm

## Key Takeaways {#slide-34}

Diversity  is how we see each other; be sure to consider intersectionality!

Equity  is how we elevate those who have been historically left behind; organization level

Inclusion  is how we treat each other; individual level

- Allyship -- an ongoing relationship between an ally and their partner, working together toward a shared goal 
- Inclusive leadership -- Visible commitment, humility, awareness of bias, curiosity about others, cultural intelligence, and effective collaboration
- Everyday inclusion -- microaffirmations

Belonging  is feeling valued, respected, seen, and heard

Having bias is natural, the key is acknowledging it and counteracting as needed

Creating a diverse, equitable, and inclusive organization benefits employees and the  business, but it takes a conscious effort to have the most impact
