## Overview of Training & DEVELOPMENT {#slide-2}

- Training is a short-term focused effort with specific, concrete goals that target a particular role or job requirement. 
- On the other hand, development is a long-term endeavor with open-ended and ongoing goals aimed at the overall progression of the individual.

## Aguinis  &  Kraiger , 2018 {#slide-6}

Benefits of Training for.....

Individual's & Teams

- Evidence suggests that training activities have a positive impact on individual and team performance (e.g. more efficient)
- Also linked to improving individual and team attitudes, motivations, and empowerment

Organizations

- Studies conducted (although few), support the fact that organizational performance is improved (e.g., profitability, effectiveness, productivity, operating revenue per employee)
- Other more indirect outcomes occur too, specifically, reduced costs, lower employee turnover,  and better organizational reputation and culture.

Society

- Most research here has been conducted by economists
- It has been found that training efforts produce improvements in the quality of the labor force, which in turn is leads to national economic growth.

## Prompt {#slide-10}

	Training and Development play pivotal roles in shaping the capabilities and progression of employees within an organization. However, this topic is often subject of debate, raising questions about the most effective strategies, their impact on organizational success, and how they should be integrated into the broader HR framework.

From the following 3 stances, choose one you align with the most and support why you align with it.

1. Traditional types training & development is irrelevant

2. Training & development is overly expensive and should require less investment from the organization, unless there is a large return on investment 

3. Well-being and motivation should be prioritized in Training & Development to be effective

## Discussion Question 1 {#slide-11}

For the stance that believes traditional types of training & development are irrelevant:

- What specific shortcomings or drawbacks of traditional training methods do proponents of this stance point out?
- Are there examples of organizations that have successfully transitioned from traditional to more modern training approaches?

## Discussion Question 2 {#slide-12}

For the stance that argues training & development is overly expensive and should require less investment unless there is a large return on investment:

- How do proponents suggest measuring the return on investment for training programs?
- Are there industries or contexts where the cost-effectiveness of training is particularly challenging?

## Discussion Question 3 {#slide-13}

For the stance prioritizing well-being and motivation in Training & Development:

- What specific strategies or practices are recommended to integrate well-being and motivation into training programs?
- Can you provide examples of organizations that have successfully implemented such holistic training approaches and seen positive outcomes?
