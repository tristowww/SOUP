## Simulations  Cascio &  Aguinis  (2018) {#slide-11}

Represent real-life situations, where trainees' decisions reflect what would happen if they were on the job.

Possible Methods:

::: {.smartart .LinedList layout="LinedList"}
**Incident Method**

- question the trainer, when ready attempt the solution
- trainer reveals all the information & compares solutions
- sketchy outline of a particular incident

**Role Playing**

- Large group splits into smaller groups & role plays same problem
- Discuss with trainer what happened in groups

**Case Method**

- situations presented to groups of trainees who identify and offer solutions. 
- Learn from each other and receive feedback on their performances
:::

::: {.smartart .LinedList layout="LinedList"}
**Task Model**

- Reach solutions through group discussion
- Group replicates it using alt. communication arrangements, only select trainees can view the object
- Trainees construct complex physical object

**Experiential Exercises**

- Incorporate elements of the case method, multiple role-playing, and team-coordination 
- Examine their responses first as individuals, then with group members, then larger group, then with trainer
:::

Research Data on Effectiveness of Online Learning

Noe (2023) and Cascio &  Aguinis  (2018)

- Declarative Knowledge : Online instruction is better for teaching facts and concepts.
- Procedural Knowledge : Both online and classroom instruction are equally effective for teaching skills.
- Learner Satisfaction : Learners are equally happy with both online and classroom instruction.
- Effectiveness of Web-based Instruction : Online instruction is more effective when learners have control over content, pace, and sequence, in longer courses, and when they can practice with feedback.
- Similar Instructional Methods : Online and classroom instruction are equally effective when they use similar teaching methods like videos, practice assignments, and tests.
- Engagement and Completion : Employees who engage more with online practice and take their time with the training get the most benefit.
- Not Suitable for Everyone : Online learning may not work well for everyone, especially those who aren\'t confident with computers.

## Procedure {#slide-39}

Scheduled for one of seven 2-day session, independent work with 8 -- 15 other trainees.

Trainees were told to proceed at their own pace but to attempt to complete the course by the end of the second day. 

Two days were selected, down from 3 days, because research suggests that computer-based training generally reduces learning time by a third.

Available to revisit the course Web site to follow-up on material in the course.

Completed a survey that included individual difference measures and pretests. 

The self-report measure of off-task attention was collected after learners completed 3 modules.

## Results {#slide-40}

Individual Differences

Negative effect for mastery orientation on practice and learning

Interaction between performance goal orientation and learning self-efficacy

situations may arise when self-efficacy is negatively related to effort exerted to learn

Learners with high performance orientation and high self-efficacy made much more extensive use of practice

Learners Choice

Learning goal orientation, delivery mode and the perception of barriers and enablers relation to motivation to learn and course outcomes

## Hypotheses {#slide-42}

![](ppt/media/image25.png "Content Placeholder 3")

- The extent to which learners view features as enablers rather than as barriers will be positively related to motivation to learn.
- LGO will be positively related to motivation to learn.
- Learners in the blended learning condition will be less likely to perceive features as enablers than learners in the classroom condition.
