**[Course Description:]{.underline}**

This course will address theories, practical issues, and research in training. The purpose will be to discuss factors and processes involved in training needs assessment, design, and evaluation as well as learning processes, individual difference and motivational factors affecting training, and selected special topics.

**[Position Papers:]{.underline}**

The in-class discussion will be based on a set of weekly readings in which you will be asked to write a **two-page maximum (double-spaced)** "position paper". In the position paper, you will convey and support *your* position on a provided discussion prompt, rather than provide a "correct" answer. These papers will be used to organize and facilitate our in-class discussion.

All position papers should be submitted online via D2L **no later than 9am on the Tuesday** before our class. This is a hard deadline (no late position papers will be accepted and will receive a score of 0). I will spend the day before our class meeting reading through and grading the position papers to organize our discussion in a way that considers the points, evidence, and arguments you have used to support your position. I will provide comments on your paper before class, so that you can reference your position paper during the discussions.

Each position paper will be graded out of **20 points** with the following anchors (20 = superior, 18 = good, 15 = acceptable, 10 = unacceptable, 5 = completely unacceptable, 0 = late). Because communicating clearly and concisely is an important skill as an I/O psychologist, these papers should be polished (largely free of typos/ illogical phrasings/ etc.) **The position paper for the week you lead the discussion should be skipped and will be dropped.**

You may use at least two of any of the articles in the provided reading for the week, no outside articles are necessary for writing the position paper. In text citations should be used, but no reference page is needed.

**[Guest Speaker Reflection Papers:]{.underline}**

We will have 3 planned guest speakers during the semester, who will talk about specific topics related to training and development. You are expected to come prepared to ask questions and be an active and respectful listener.

After each guest speaker, you will complete a **1-2 page (double-spaced)** reflection paper on the content of the guest speaker's presentation. All position papers should be submitted online via D2L one week after the discussion **no later than 9am on the next Wednesday before class**. These papers are open-ended and provide me with insight as to what you gained from the speaker and what you took away.

These reflection papers should not merely summarize the talk but should delve into some of the following: your reactions, insight, thoughts, relevant experiences, relevant articles, how it relates to other concepts you have learned, new perspectives you have gained, etc.

They will be graded out of **20 points**, full points are granted as long as you write a paper that is on topic, has an organizational structure, and has some substantial content related to the guest speaker's presentation.

**[Discussion Participation:]{.underline}**

When you are not leading the discussion for the week, you are expected to read the articles for the week (the book chapters are optional if you are not leading) and complete the position paper before class. You should come prepared to actively participate in discussion based on the articles and position paper, as well as follow up questions from the student leading the discussion. You will be graded for your participation during each discussion you are not leading and will receive **10 points** for actively participating and respectfully listening during class.

**[Leading Discussion:]{.underline}**

You are responsible for leading a discussion topic for one class. This can be achieved by preparing a PowerPoint presentation (or other similar visual means) that includes the following components:

1.  Summarize the weekly topic (40 points):

    - You can use the provided book chapters and content, the articles, or outside material.

    - This is basically a short summary lecture/introduction to the weekly topic.

2.  Summary of the weekly articles (40 points):

    - This is just a quick overview covering the main takeaways from the articles relating to the weekly topic.

3.  Follow up discussion questions/prompts (20 points):

    - Have a couple (about 2-3) follow up points to stimulate the discussion based on the position paper prompt, the articles, or the topic in general.

OR

3.  Interactive activity (20 point):

    - This can include the following: case study, debate, video, tangible example material, code, personal experience, etc.

**[General Attendance and Participation:]{.underline}**

Because this is a discussion-based course, you are expected to attend classes and participate in all classes. This includes having completed the readings and position papers prior to a given class period and arriving on time and ready to discuss them.

If you have an extenuating circumstance that requires you to miss class, please let me know at least a day in advance, if possible. These situations will not impact your grade negatively and we will work together to provide you with a suitable make up option.
