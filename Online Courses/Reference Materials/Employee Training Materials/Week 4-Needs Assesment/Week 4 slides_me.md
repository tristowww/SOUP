## Organizational Analysis {#slide-11}

-   Companies needs to decide if they will use their own resources to develop training or if it will be outsourced 
-  Peer and manager support is crucial for fostering enthusiasm and motivation to attend training 

Questions to consider asking: 

How might training content impact our employee relationships?

How does this program align with the strategic needs of the business?

What features of the environment might interfere with the training? 

11

## Person Analysis {#slide-12}

-  A gap analysis can be used to determine what is responsible for differences between employee's current and expected performance 
- 
-  An employee's "readiness for training" refers to whether employees have the personal characteristic (ability, attitudes, beliefs, and motivation) necessary to learn  program content and apply it on the job, and whether the work environment will facilitate learning and not interfere with performance

<!-- -->

- Interviews can be used to assess these factors 

12

## Task Analysis {#slide-13}

Task analysis determine the task completed in a certain job and the KSAOs necessary to complete them 

Knowledge includes facts or procedures 

Skills indicates competency in performing a task 

Ability includes the physical and mental capacities to perform a task 

Other refers to the conditions under which the task is performed

## Brown (2002) {#slide-26}

Summary:

-   It is important to effectively present findings of needs assessments to stakeholders and decision-makers
-  Conducting a training needs assessment can be time-consuming and resource-intensive, but there are long-term benefits outweighing the initial costs 
-  Needs assessments should be done as an ongoing process

## Van  Eerde  et al., (2008) {#slide-27}

The Mediating Role of Training Utility in the Relationship Between Training Needs Assessment and Organizational Effectiveness

Examines utility of training as a mediator for the relationship btw training needs assessment comprehensiveness and org effectiveness

- Training Utility:

<!-- -->

- Perceived applicability and relevance of training to job performance
- Utility was a partial mediator between TNA comprehensiveness and organizational effectiveness.

27

## Van  Eerde  et al., (2008) {#slide-28}

Findings:

- TNA comprehensiveness directly increased organizational effectiveness .

- TNA comprehensiveness also increased training utility , which in turn  increased organizational effectiveness .
- 
- Both paths were significant , meaning  TNA affected effectiveness directly and indirectly through utility . 

28

TNA Comprehensiveness → Organizational Effectiveness (direct effect)

TNA Comprehensiveness → Training Utility → Organizational Effectiveness (indirect effect)
