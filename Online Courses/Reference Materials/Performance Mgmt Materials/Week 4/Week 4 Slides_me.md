## Performance Management Measurement {#slide-7}

Results-based metrics:  Focus on outcomes (e.g., sales, output, timeliness)

Pros: Clear accountability, easy to link to strategy

Cons: May ignore "how" work gets done

Behavior-based metrics:  Focus on competencies & interpersonal skills

Pros: Captures teamwork, ethics, customer service

Cons: Harder to measure consistently

Best practice:  Balance both for fairness and effectiveness ( Pulakos , 2009)

## Performance Rating Distortions {#slide-10}

- Even with accurate criteria and effective measurement, ratings can be an ineffective means of capturing an employee's performance
- Human biases and psychological tendencies can lead to inaccurate ratings (rating errors)
- Rating Distortions can be:

<!-- -->

- Simple errors
- Intentional Distortions
- Systematic distortions

10

## Common Rating Distortions {#slide-11}

11

::: {.smartart .ChevronBlockProcess layout="ChevronBlockProcess"}
**Central Tendency Error**

- Tendency to use the midpoint of the scale 

**Halo Error**

- Tendency to rate an employee the same on all dimensions of performance

**Leniency/severity Errors**

- Tendency to assign very extreme scores on either end of the rating scale
:::

## Relative Vs. Absolute Measurement {#slide-12}

::: {.smartart .list1 layout="list1"}
**Absolute Measures**

- Behaviorally-Anchored Rating Scales (BARS)
- Graphic rating scales

**Relative Measures**

- Employees are judged in comparison to each other

**Employees are judged in comparison to each other**

- Simple ranking
- Forced distribution
- Paired comparisons
:::

## Viswesvaran  et al., 2005 {#slide-13}

Is there a general factor to ratings of job performance?

- Reviewing if we can capture a general concept of true job performance when controlling for halo, leniency, random error, and transient error
- The influence of halo error on the true score of performance dimensions

- Around 60% of the variance in performance ratings can be explained by a single general performance factor

## Sackett et al. (1988) {#slide-14}

- Distinction btw typical vs. maximum performance in criteria
- May vary across jobs 
- Typical and max performance maintain a low to modest correlation (.16-.36)
- We cannot measure these the same

<!-- -->

- Ability best predicts maximum performance
- Motivation best for typical performance prediction

## Fenech et al. (2022) {#slide-15}

Investigates how performance appraisal perpetuate gender inequality in orgs

- Task vs. Behavioral performance reflect women in an inferior position to men

<!-- -->

- Women focus on tangible work leading to lower behavioral scores based on social activities compared to men 

<!-- -->

- Stems from traditionally gendered hierarchies and socialization 
- Importance of looking at the effect of different performance on each other and not just outcomes
