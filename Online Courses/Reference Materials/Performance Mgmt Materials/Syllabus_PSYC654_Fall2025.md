**[Course Objectives:]{.underline}**

This course is designed to examine theory, research, and applications of the performance management process and how it is connected to other organizational activities. Through discussion, lecture, activities, and an external client project, professional skills needed to translate research-based knowledge into successful organizational practices will be developed.

The overall goal of this course is to familiarize you with a range performance appraisal and management topics through academic article readings, discussions, in class activities and demonstrations, and a project.

**[By the end of this course, students should be able to:]{.underline}**

- Critique the design, methodology, and results of empirical articles in performance management topics.

- Identify and explain theories and principles related to the topics in the course.

- Integrate theories and principles in a holistic manner.

- Integrate the topics covered in the course in a real-world setting.

**[Client Project:]{.underline}**

This class includes an external project component. That is, we will be working on a collaborative project with an external client. The project is with the Talent Development program in the department of human resources at Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University. They switched to a new performance management system last year and we helped conduct a needs assessment for employee training. We are expanding on that project and focusing on assessing the needs of supervisors who will be utilizing the performance management system to conduct employee performance appraisals and create employee development goals.

This project will become more defined as we communicate with the client and their needs for the project. There *should* be a final presentation to the client. If there is not an opportunity to present to the client, we will practice presenting as a class or create a report for the client. A peer evaluation process will be used to assist in grading this group project. [The project will be graded based on the semester's effort and final project for a total of 60 pts. The peer evaluation will factor into the remaining 40 pts for a total possible 100 pts for the client project.]{.underline}
