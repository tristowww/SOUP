## Management by Objective (MBO) {#slide-19}

- Employee input
- Alignment with org goals
- Increased engagement and motivation

<!-- -->

-  Cons

<!-- -->

- Time intensive
- Overemphasis on goals
- Long term orientation
- Lack of goal quality
- Mangers need training and skills
- Individual focused

## Determining Overall Ratings {#slide-25}

-   Judgement-based approach  -- holistic rater judgement of performance 
-   Mechanical approach  -- weighted average of ratings across dimensions
-  Hybrid models  -- numeric ratings combined with narrative feedback

Implications:

-  Impacts pay, promotion, terminations, demotions, and legal defensibility 

25

## Weighting Performance Dimensions {#slide-26}

-  Equal weighting
-   Organizational priorities
-  Role-specific needs
-  Percentage based

<!-- -->

- If Quality = 40%, Speed = 30%, Communication = 30%, total score can be calculated by multiplying performance ratings by these percentages.

26

## Quantitate vs. Qualitative Measures {#slide-27}

::: {.smartart .hList1 layout="hList1"}
**Qualititative**

- Pros: context based, developmental, details
- Cons: subjective, time-consuming
- Narrative feedback, behavioral observation

**Quantitative**

- Cons: lacks details or nuance
- Pros: easy to compare, objective
- Sales numbers, completed projects
:::

27

## Combining Qualitative and Quantitative Data {#slide-28}

-  Hybrid approach using narrative comments to explain quantitative scores

<!-- -->

- Ex: Use sales figures to measure performance but include narrative feedback to provide context for why targets were or weren't met.

## Combining Quantitative Scores {#slide-30}

30

::: {.smartart .list1 layout="list1"}
**Averaging**

- Mean of scores of different dimensions
- Ex: Average the scores of timeliness (4), quality (3), and teamwork (5) = Final score of 4

**Summing**

- Sum scores to capture overall performance contributions

**Normalization**

- Adjusting ratings when combining different scales
:::

31

::: {.smartart .process4 layout="process4"}

- Pros: more holistic, developmental, strategic alignment

**Traditional (ranking, graphic ratings scales, critical incidents, narrative essays)**

- Cons: subjective, outdated, less developmental
- Pros: simple, inexpensive

## Spence & Keeping (2010) {#slide-35}

Performance ratings may be distorted by non-performance factors

-  Avoiding negative consequences
-  Organizational norms
-  Self interest
- 
-  Using policy-capturing methods, they found that managers' ratings were significantly influenced by all 3 factors independent of actual performance
