## Relevant Cases {#slide-18}

Brito v. Zia Company (1973)

-  Employees were terminated based on subjective performance evals, leading to allegations of discrimination

<!-- -->

- Court found that the evals lacked proper validation and resulted in discriminatory outcomes

## Relevant Cases {#slide-19}

Albemarle Paper Co. v. Moody (1975)

-  Albemarle Paper Co. used employment tests (Beta Examination & Wonderlic Personnel Test) without proper validation, leading to a discrete impact on African American employees.

<!-- -->

- Employment tests must be job-related and validated to ensure they do not discriminate against protected groups

## Legally Defensible Performance Management ( Pulakos , 2009) {#slide-20}

-  Well organized and communicated in advance
-  Performance ratings should be job-related
-  Raters should have observed what they are rating
-  Raters should be trained (to rate, record, for bias, for  ethics)
-  Appraisals should be discussed with employees 
-  Employees should be given a formal system to appeal or express disagreement
-  Ratings should not be used as punishment
-  Ratings should be timely 
-  Ratings should be based on documented behavior

## Sources of Algorithmic Bias {#slide-34}

Historical Bias:

When algorithms are trained on data that reflects societal inequities

![](ppt/media/image105.jpg "Picture 39")

Representation Bias: 

When certain groups or characteristics are underrepresented overrepresented in the training data 

![](ppt/media/image105.jpg "Picture 40")

Technical Bias:

Bias that stems from the technical design of an algorithm

![](ppt/media/image105.jpg "Picture 41")

Emergent Bias: 

Bias that arises after an algorithm is deployed or implemented for use due to changes in norms,  behavior , or context
