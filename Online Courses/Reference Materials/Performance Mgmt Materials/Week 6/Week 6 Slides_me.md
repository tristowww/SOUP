## Vroom's Expectancy Theory (1960) {#slide-11}

- Motivation depends on how much we want something and how likely we are to get it
- Assumptions:

<!-- -->

- Behavior is determined by a combo of personal and environmental factors
- People make decisions about their own behavior in organizations
- Different people have different types of needs, desires, and goals
- People choose among alternatives of behaviors in selecting one that leads to a desired outcome

## Vroom's Expectancy Theory (1960) {#slide-12}

- Suggests that motivation depends on 3 factors: 
- Expectancy:  the belief that increased effort will lead to increased performance
- Instrumentality:  the belief that if you perform well, you will receive a certain outcome
- Valence:  the value an individual places on the outcome or reward

## Equity Theory (Adams, 1963) {#slide-14}

- People are motivated to seek social equity in the rewards they receive for performance
- Individual\'s view of the value of rewards (outcomes) and inputs of effort as ratios and make subjective comparisons of themselves to other people

## Bauwens et al., 2019 {#slide-25}

Perceptions of fairness in performance management systems impact burnout and OCBs in academic employees

- Fair PM systems  (distributive, procedural, interactional justice) → ↓  burnout  & ↑  OCB .
- Burnout mediates  the fairness--OCB link.

- Transparent outcomes + respectful treatment protect well-being and encourage discretionary effort.

<!-- -->

- Fair PM → less emotional exhaustion and more discretionary effort.
- OCBs increased through promotion of employee well-being and positive work environment
