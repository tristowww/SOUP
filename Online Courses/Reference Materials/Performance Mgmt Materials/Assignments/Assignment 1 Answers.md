**[Case:]{.mark}**

[RetailCo is a national retail chain with over 200 stores across the country. The company\'s strategic objectives focus on increasing customer satisfaction, driving sales growth, and improving operational efficiency. RetailCo relies on a traditional performance appraisal system to evaluate its employees, including sales associates, supervisors, and store managers. The appraisal system is centered around annual performance reviews where employees are rated based on key performance indicators (KPIs) such as sales targets, attendance, teamwork, and customer service ratings (collected through monthly customer surveys and mystery shopper reports).]{.mark}

[Managers use a 5-point rating scale to assess employee performance, and these ratings significantly influence decisions related to promotions, pay raises, and recognition. Feedback in the current system is typically provided only during these annual review sessions, where managers discuss the ratings with employees, highlighting strengths and identifying areas for improvement. However, the feedback is often retrospective, focusing primarily on past performance with limited guidance on future development.]{.mark}

[To incentivize teams, RetailCo has implemented a unique approach: stores that meet or exceed performance targets are rewarded with a pizza party at the end of the month. This team-based incentive aims to boost morale and foster a sense of collective achievement among employees.]{.mark}

[Despite its intentions, the performance appraisal system at RetailCo faces several challenges. Managers often struggle to recall employee performance accurately over the course of the year, leading to generalized ratings that fail to reflect true individual performance. Recent incidents tend to be over-weighted in evaluations, while consistent day-to-day performance is often forgotten. The yearly feedback leaves employees feeling disconnected from their performance goals and lacking the guidance needed from their managers to improve. Additionally, the team reward of the pizza party tends to be unrelated to specific performance goals and overshadows individual contributions. As a result, managers feel unable to provide effective feedback to motivate high-potential employees, leading to delayed promotions and increased turnover among top performers.]{.mark}

[RetailCo should implement a cascading goal system that links organizational strategy to individual performance (lecture content on strategic alignment). Task performance should include sales results and customer service quality, while contextual performance should measure teamwork behaviors, helping colleagues, organizational citizenship behaviors, and initiative-taking that support operational efficiency. Given Dalal et al.\'s (2020) findings about performance variability, RetailCo should implement continuous feedback systems with monthly check-ins and quarterly formal reviews rather than annual assessments.]{.mark}

4.  **[Based on the case details and Murphy\'s (2008) research on performance rating problems, identify the specific types of bias affecting RetailCo\'s ratings (e.g., recency effects, memory limitations, halo effects). How can RetailCo implement systematic solutions to ensure more fair and accurate evaluations?]{.mark}**

[Key Points Students Should Address:]{.mark}

[Murphy\'s (2008) mediated model best explains RetailCo\'s rating problems. The case demonstrates multiple systematic biases: recency effects (recent incidents over-weighted), availability bias (memorable events vs. consistent performance), and halo effects (generalized ratings). These aren\'t random measurement errors but systematic distortions in the rating process.]{.mark}

[The annual review cycle exacerbates memory limitations, leading to what Murphy identifies as systematic rather than random rating problems. Managers may also engage in intentional distortion to avoid difficult conversations or maintain team harmony (mediated model). The multifactor model suggests that non-performance factors (manager recall ability, time pressures, relationship quality) significantly influence ratings beyond actual employee performance.]{.mark}

[Systematic Solutions:]{.mark}

- [Multiple measurement points throughout the year to reduce memory bias]{.mark}

- [Behavioral documentation systems for ongoing performance tracking]{.mark}

- [360-degree feedback to provide multiple perspectives]{.mark}

- [Calibration sessions among managers to ensure consistency]{.mark}

- [Training on cognitive biases and rating accuracy]{.mark}

- [Behaviorally anchored rating scales (BARS) to improve precision]{.mark}

- [Separate raters for developmental vs. administrative purposes]{.mark}

- [Technology platforms for continuous performance data collection]{.mark}
