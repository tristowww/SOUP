## Differentiator

Most job search courses teach surface-level resume tips and generic interview advice. This course is taught by someone who designs the hiring systems candidates are trying to navigate. Teresa's I/O psychology research — including structured interview design (Campion's 15 components), validity evidence for every selection method, and her dissertation on deceptive language in job advertisements — gives candidates an insider's understanding of what employers actually measure, how AI screens applications, and how to spot red-flag job postings before accepting an offer.

## MVP Scope (5 Core Modules)

1. **How Hiring Actually Works** - The selection system from the inside: what employers measure, how decisions are made, and why "culture fit" is often code for bias
2. **Resume Science** - What validity research says about resumes, how ATS systems parse applications, and evidence-based strategies for getting past the screen
3. **Interview Mastery** - How structured interviews are scored (Campion's 15 components from the employer side), the STAR method grounded in behavioral research, and how to handle every interview format
4. **Surviving Assessments** - Cognitive ability tests, personality inventories, SJTs, and work simulations: what they measure, how they're scored, and evidence-based preparation strategies
5. **Decoding Job Ads** - Teresa's dissertation research applied: NLP-identified linguistic markers of deception in job postings, red flags in employer communication, and a framework for evaluating whether a job is what it claims to be
