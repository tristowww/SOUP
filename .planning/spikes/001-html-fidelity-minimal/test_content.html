Module 1: Structured Interviews

Why this matters

Structured interviews predict job performance roughly twice as well as unstructured interviews (Schmidt & Hunter, 1998). If you take one thing from this course, take this.

By the end of this lesson you will:
  • Define structured interviewing
  • Identify three core components
  • Draft a behavioral question

18 minutes reading + 12 minutes exercise

Example behavioral question

Q: Tell me about a time you disagreed with a hiring
   decision. What did you do?

Rubric (0-5):
  5 — Named specific decision, voiced concern through
      formal channel, followed up after resolution.
  3 — Voiced concern but did not follow up.
  0 — Did not voice concern.

When you see a candidate struggle with this, don't fill the silence. Let them think.
