Roasters

US specialty roasters scraped via the Shopify JSON API. Product descriptions are filtered to exclude merchandise, then tasting note phrases are extracted from structured HTML elements, regex patterns, and comma-separated flavor lists.

Importers

10 active sources · 58 phrases · 3 planned

Green coffee importers sit between producer and roaster. Their language bridges origin descriptions and market vocabulary. Many importers gate offering lists behind logins or PDFs, making web scraping harder — the broad extraction function captures what's publicly available.

Institutions

8 web sources + 5 local SCA docs · 1,015 phrases · 2 planned

Coffee institutions codify the vocabulary that roasters and cuppers use. Their standards documents, flavor wheels, and educational content define the official sensory language — making them essential for understanding where the terminology originates.

Active — Web Sources

SCA (Main + Research + 25 Magazine)

Active
sca.coffee
Flavor wheel page, sensory analysis, green coffee standards, cupping form, processing, arabica varieties, water quality, brewing standards. Research portal and 25 Magazine articles.
14 paths scraped across 3 subdomains
The SCA defines the vocabulary. Their standards are the source of truth for specialty coffee sensory language.

Active — Local SCA Documents
Included

SCA-103 Descriptive Assessment (2024)

The vocabulary standard. Three lexicons (olfactory, taste, mouthfeel) with intensity ratings. CATA methodology for sensory attributes. 9 chunks.

Included

SCA-A System to Assess Coffee Value (2024)

Master framework. Seven cupping sections (fragrance, aroma, flavor, aftertaste, acidity, sweetness, mouthfeel). Sensory science context. 64 chunks.

Included

Attributes Framework Whitepaper (2021)

Theoretical grounding. Flavor Wheel as attribute collection. Attribute-pricing correlation. Preference mapping. 7 chunks.

Included

CVAS Evolution Report (2022)

Why vocabulary was restructured. User perception survey (1,575 respondents). Which sensory terms caused confusion. 20 chunks.

Included

SCA-104 Affective Assessment (2024)

Preference vs. description distinction. Defect categorization. How sensory perception maps to quality scores. 9 chunks.

Skipped

SCA-102 Sample Preparation (2024)

Brewing protocol, equipment specs, water parameters. No sensory vocabulary.

Skipped

SCA-105 Extrinsic Assessment (2025)

Origin, certifications, environmental/social attributes. No flavor language.

Skipped

Extrinsic Assessment Beta Proposal (2024)

Research report on non-sensory attribute development. No flavor vocabulary.

Producers
