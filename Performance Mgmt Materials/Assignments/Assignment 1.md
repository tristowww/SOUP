**[Case:]{.mark}**

[RetailCo is a national retail chain with over 200 stores across the country. The company\'s strategic objectives focus on increasing customer satisfaction, driving sales growth, and improving operational efficiency. RetailCo relies on a traditional performance appraisal system to evaluate its employees, including sales associates, supervisors, and store managers. The appraisal system is centered around annual performance reviews where employees are rated based on key performance indicators (KPIs) such as sales targets, attendance, teamwork, and customer service ratings (collected through monthly customer surveys and mystery shopper reports).]{.mark}

[Managers use a 5-point rating scale to assess employee performance, and these ratings significantly influence decisions related to promotions, pay raises, and recognition. Feedback in the current system is typically provided only during these annual review sessions, where managers discuss the ratings with employees, highlighting strengths and identifying areas for improvement. However, the feedback is often retrospective, focusing primarily on past performance with limited guidance on future development.]{.mark}

[To incentivize teams, RetailCo has implemented a unique approach: stores that meet or exceed performance targets are rewarded with a pizza party at the end of the month. This team-based incentive aims to boost morale and foster a sense of collective achievement among employees.]{.mark}

[Despite its intentions, the performance appraisal system at RetailCo faces several challenges. Managers often struggle to recall employee performance accurately over the course of the year, leading to generalized ratings that fail to reflect true individual performance. Recent incidents tend to be over-weighted in evaluations, while consistent day-to-day performance is often forgotten. The yearly feedback leaves employees feeling disconnected from their performance goals and lacking the guidance needed from their managers to improve. Additionally, the team reward of the pizza party tends to be unrelated to specific performance goals and overshadows individual contributions. As a result, managers feel unable to provide effective feedback to motivate high-potential employees, leading to delayed promotions and increased turnover among top performers.]{.mark}
