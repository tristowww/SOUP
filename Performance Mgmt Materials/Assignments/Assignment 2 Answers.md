4.  **[Measurement Scale Considerations:]{.mark}**

[Each measurement scale has its own advantages and challenges that impact its appropriateness in various performance management contexts. Each scale also is susceptible to specific biases and rating errors that can affect the accuracy and fairness of performance evaluations. Understanding these nuances is crucial for selecting and implementing the most appropriate measurement method in any given setting. Respond to the following questions based on these considerations:]{.mark}

1.  [How do the three measurement scales (BARS, GRS, RPM) differ in how they define and describe your chosen performance criteria?]{.mark}

> • **BARS**: Provides **specific behavioral anchors**, clear expectations, more detailed.
>
> • **GRS**: Broader categories, less specificity, open to interpretation.

• **RPM**: Relies on **comparisons across individuals/samples**, not absolute standards.

2.  What are the main advantages of each measurement scale?

> • **BARS**: Increases clarity, reduces ambiguity, improves inter-rater reliability.

• **GRS**: Easy to administer, quick, less time-consuming.

• **RPM**: Captures relative performance, useful for distinguishing among many performers.

3.  What biases and/or rating errors are most likely with each scale?

> • **BARS**: Time-consuming to develop; may still face **leniency or severity bias**.

• **GRS**: Susceptible to **halo effect, central tendency, and rater idiosyncrasies**.

• **RPM**: Vulnerable to **contrast effects** (comparisons depend on who is in the sample).

4.  [How does the specificity of the criteria/dimensions influence the rating process and the quality of evaluations?]{.mark}

> • Clearer criteria increase **consistency** across raters.

• Specific dimensions improve **validity** by ensuring ratings map onto defined behaviors.

• Ambiguous dimensions reduce fairness, create rater disagreement, and lower reliability.
