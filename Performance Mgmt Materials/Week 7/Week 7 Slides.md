## Latham & Locke, 2017 {#slide-29}

Goals must be specific and challenging

- Goal setting remains one of the most robust motivation interventions across decades.
- Effective goals = specific, challenging, accepted, feedback-supported.
- New evidence: Goal framing & priming (subconscious cues matter), learning vs. performance goals (learning goals help under uncertainty).
- Context moderators: feedback quality, task complexity, ability level, culture.
- Practical: Avoid "too many" goals; integrate with coaching and development plans.

29

## Latham & Locke, 2017 {#slide-30}

Why Goal Setting Sometimes Fails

- Misaligned with ability/resources → frustration.
- Focused only on outcomes → unethical shortcuts or burnout.
- Lack of feedback → employees can't self-correct.
- High uncertainty → learning goals outperform performance goals.
