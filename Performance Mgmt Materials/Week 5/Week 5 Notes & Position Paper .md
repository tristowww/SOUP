This week's readings delve into the varied approaches to performance appraisal systems, including different ratings methods highlighted in Aggarwal & Thakur (2013) and Brutus (2010), which examines numeric and narrative styles of evaluation. Additionally, the topics discussed in Kondrasuk (2012) and Spence & Keeping (2010) offer further insights into nuanced positive and negative considerations of employee assessments.

Drawing from the insights in these articles, which approach to performance appraisal -- numeric ratings or written comments -- offers greater benefit to employees? Back up your position by discussing why you believe your chosen method better supports employees.

\- Review of performance appraisal systems

-Traditional methods

\- ranking method (best to worst on a trait)

-graphic rating scales (lists traits and an associated performance and what it looks like as a behavior and the employee is rated based on the score that best represents their behavior)

-critical incidents method (collect incidents of employee behavior that are examples of good and bad work performance)

-narrative essays (explanation of employee strengths and weaknesss in general)

-modern methods

\- management by objectives (setting objective and creating an action plan to implement to provide performance feedback and development)-first performance management to set goals

-behaviorally anchored rating scales (BARS-handles issues of validity and reliability of rating scales helps the rater focus on specific desirable and undesirable behaviors by giving example work behaviors as examples

-HR accounting- focus on development of employees

-Assessment centers- testing job performance by having employees do their job in controlled setting

-360 degree- superior, collegues, subordinates, customers, suppliers unique perspectives to initiate behavioral change

-720 degree- focus on upper level management and is more personalized customers or stakeholder/investors,

-summarizes advantages and disadvantages

**Hannah Ashby**

\- overall position: written comments offer a more beneficial approach to performance appraisals

\- Concerns with numeric ratings: rating distortions due to social desirability bias, lack substantial qualitative backing, impersonal and devoid of constructive feedback

-advantages of written comments: personalized developmental feedback, open communication and transparency, substantial context to the performance being rated

-evaluators can skew numeric values more and must have more evidence for written comments and are forced to think harder
