- **Discussion Point:** How does the integration of feedback from supervisors, peers, subordinates, and self-assessment influence the validity and effectiveness of the 360-degree system?

- **Discussion Point:** Consider the quality of feedback received. How can the quality be measured, and what impact does it have on the overall effectiveness of the system?

**3. Behavioral Change and Improvement:**

- **Discussion Point:** How can organizations measure the extent to which feedback is leading to observable behavioral change and improvement in performance?

- **Discussion Point:** Evaluate the impact of potential rater biases. How can the 360-degree system mitigate these, and what measures can assess the level of objectivity?

**5. Feedback Utilization:**

- **Discussion Point:** Assess how feedback is utilized by the recipient. What metrics can be employed to measure the effective utilization of feedback for personal and professional development?

**6. Organizational Impact:**

- **Discussion Point:** Evaluate the macro-level impact of 360-degree feedback on organizational culture, productivity, and employee engagement.

- **Discussion Point:** Explore the psychometric properties of the feedback tool. How can reliability, validity, and fairness be ensured and measured?

**8. Technology and Data Analytics:**

- **Discussion Point:** How does the integration of technology and data analytics enhance the precision and actionable insights derived from the 360-degree feedback?

- **Extension:** Assess the role of AI and machine learning in enhancing feedback analysis and personalized action plans.

**9. Legal and Ethical Considerations:**

- **Discussion Point:** Explore the legal and ethical considerations in implementing a 360-degree feedback system. How can these be addressed to ensure fairness and compliance?

**10. Customization vs. Standardization:**

- **Discussion Point:** Evaluate the balance between customization and standardization in the feedback tool. How can organizations ensure that the tool is tailored yet consistent?

- **Extension:** Discuss the implications of customization on the comparability and consistency of feedback across the organization.

**Reliability, Validity, and Effectiveness of a 360-degree feedback system**

1.  **Establish a framework**

    a.  **Criterion definition**

        i.  **Clearly define the criterion you are interested in measuring (what is good performance)**

    b.  **Instrument development**

        i.  **How are you measuring the criterion? Make sure the questions are aimed at assessing the criterion then test it for reliability and validity and effectiveness**

2.  **Reliability Measurement**

    a.  **Internal consistency**

        i.  **Cronbach alpha: how closely related a set of items. Consistency of respondent's answers, higher coefficient indicates that the items are measuring the same construct of interest (does it measure what you think it should measure)**

    b.  **Test-retest reliability**

        i.  **Assessing the stability of the measurement instrument, does time impact the responses?**

    c.  **Inter-rater reliability**

        i.  **Important in 360-degree feedback with multiple raters. Ensures there is consensus in the evaluation, reliability across different observers.**

3.  **Validity assessment (cant be valid without reliability)**

    a.  **Content validity**

        i.  **Does it cover all the components of the intent?**

    b.  **Criterion-related validity**

        i.  **Correlation between test scores and an external standard or criterion**

            1.  **Predictive validity**

            2.  **Concurrent validity -- measured at the same time (not the same employees)**

    c.  **Construct validity**

        i.  **The tool is measuring what it is supposed to be measuring. Correlating with established measures, factor analysis to assess internal structure.**

4.  **Effectiveness evaluation**

    a.  **Feedback quality**

        i.  **Is the feedback provided to employees actionable and constructive. Does it contain steps for improvement that identify areas of strength and development?**

    b.  **Developmental impact/behavior change**

        i.  **Assess if individuals are making progress post-feedback. Follow up assessments or other performance metrics to confirm behavioral changes and skill enhancement**

    c.  **Organizational outcomes**

        i.  **Broader impacts like enhanced team performance, improved employee engagement, increased retention, reduced turnover, change of culture, is the 360-degree system related to organizational objective?**

5.  **How to achieve some of these goals**

**\-\--can be valid but not effective**

- **Validity refers to does it measure what it is supposed to, predict what it should for specific competencies, skills, behaviors**

- **Effectiveness refers to if the tool has an impact on enhancing individual and organizational performance, fostering development, and initiating positive change**

- **Can be valid but not effective**

  - **Feedback is not specific enough to initiate change and is not actionable**

  - **Feedback is delivered in a poor manner or not timely**

  - **There is not support in the organization or from manager to allow employees to change based on feedback**

  - **Leadership does not encourage feedback or trust and engagement in the process**

  - **No resources for employees to change (training not supported for development**

  - **No follow up on the results of the 360-degree feedback system**

  - **Culture of resistance to change**

  - **Employees respond negatively to the feedback**

- **Can be fixed by**

  - **Org support**

  - **Communication**

  - **Continuous feedback and efforts towards improvement**

  - **Training and development opportunities**
