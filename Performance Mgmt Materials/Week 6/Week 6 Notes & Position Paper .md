**Kuvaas, B. (2006). Performance appraisal satisfaction and employee outcomes: mediating and moderating roles of work motivation. *The International Journal of Human Resource Management*, *17*(3), 504-522. <https://doi.org/10.1080/09585190500521581>**

- Satisfaction with the performance appraisal and work performance, intent to turnover and org commitment

- Mediated and moderated by intrinsic employee work motivation

- In a mediated relationship, the independent variable (employee satisfaction with the performance appraisal) affects the mediator variable (intrinsic work motivation), which in turn affects the dependent variable (work performance). The mediation effect can be diagrammed as follows: So in this case, one might hypothesize that employees who are more satisfied with their performance appraisals are more intrinsically motivated at work, which then leads to better work performance.

- In your scenario, intrinsic work motivation is also acting as a moderator, which means it can influence the strength or direction of the relationship between employee satisfaction with the performance appraisal and work performance.

- The moderating effect can be conceptualized as an interaction between the independent variable and the moderator on the dependent variable:

- In this case, the effect of satisfaction with the performance appraisal on work performance may be stronger or weaker depending on the level of intrinsic work motivation. For instance, the positive effect of satisfaction with performance appraisal on work performance might be stronger for individuals with high levels of intrinsic motivation compared to those with low levels of intrinsic motivation.

- When it is stated that intrinsic work motivation is both mediating and moderating the relationship between employee satisfaction with performance appraisal and work performance, it suggests a more complex model where intrinsic work motivation is playing a dual role in explaining how and under what conditions satisfaction with the performance appraisal affects work performance. This might imply a conditional process model where both the mediation and moderation are occurring, potentially at different stages or in different ways.

- When we combine both mediation and moderation, the intrinsic motivation is acting as a bridge (connecting appraisal satisfaction and work performance) and at the same time a magnifier (adjusting the strength of this connection).

- So, the way appraisal satisfaction leads to better performance isn\'t just a straight path, but one that can vary in strength depending on the level of an employee\'s intrinsic motivation. It\'s like walking on a bridge (mediation) where the length of the bridge can change (moderation) based on how motivated an individual is.

[https://doi.org/10.1037/a0038334](https://psycnet.apa.org/doi/10.1037/a0038334)**

\- older employee development, career growth recognition (goal setting)

\- characteristics of performance feedback and reactions is moderated by age

Our findings indicate that older employees tend to be more attuned to the social aspects of the feedback they receive, yet less concerned about how this feedback can be practically applied to their roles, compared to their younger counterparts. Furthermore, we noticed that older employees responded more positively to feedback that was favorable and well-delivered, while younger employees valued the actual quality and usefulness of the feedback more.
