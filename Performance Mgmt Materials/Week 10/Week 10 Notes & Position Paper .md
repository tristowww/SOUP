**[Week 10 (10/25): Performance Appraisal in Teams]{.underline}**

**Scott, S. G., & Einstein, W. O. (2001). Strategic performance appraisal in team-based organizations: One size does not fit all. *The Academy of Management Executive*, *15*(2), 107-116. <https://doi.org/10.5465/ame.2001.4614990>**

- This article emphasizes the complexity of performance appraisal in team-based organizations. It argues against a "one size fits all" approach, pointing out the necessity for custom-tailored strategies that cater to the unique dynamics and needs of different teams to effectively measure and enhance performance.

**Brumback, G. B. (2003). Blending "we/me" in performance management. *Team Performance Management: An International Journal*, *9*(7/8), 167-173. <https://doi.org/10.1108/13527590310507417>**

- Brumback introduces the concept of blending \"we/me\" in performance management. The article elaborates on the balance between individual and collective performance evaluations. It underscores the need for a comprehensive approach that evaluates both individual contributions and collective team performance to ensure organizational effectiveness.

**Beersma, B., Hollenbeck, J. R., Humphrey, S. E., Moon, H., Conlon, D. E., & Ilgen, D. R. (2003). Cooperation, competition, and team performance: Toward a contingency approach. *Academy of Management Journal*, *46*(5), 572-590. <https://doi.org/10.5465/30040650>**

- This piece of literature takes a detailed look at the dynamics of cooperation and competition within teams and their influence on team performance. The authors propose a contingency approach, suggesting that the effects of cooperation and competition on team performance are contingent upon specific situational factors.
